SYNCLINAL
Syn*cli"nal, a. Etym: [Gr.

1. Inclined downward from opposite directions, so as to meet in a
common point or line.

2. (Geol.)

Defn: Formed by strata dipping toward a common line or plane; as, a
synclinal trough or valley; a synclinal fold; -- opposed to
anticlinal.

Note: A downward flexure in the case of folded rocks makes a
synclinal axis, and the alternating upward flexure an anticlinal
axis.
SYNCLINAL

=====
Syn*cli"nal, n. (Geol.)
