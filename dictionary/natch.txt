NATCH
Natch, n. Etym: [OF. nache fesse, LL. natica, from L. natis the rump,
buttocks. Cf. Aitchbone.]

Defn: The rump of beef; esp., the lower and back part of the rump.
Natch bone, the edgebone, or aitchbone, in beef.
