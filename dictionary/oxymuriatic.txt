OXYMURIATIC
Ox`y*mu`ri*at"ic, a. Etym: [Oxy- (a) + muriatic: cf. F.
oxymuriatique.] (Chem.)

Defn: Pertaining to, or consisting of, oxygen and muriatic acid, that
is, hydrochloric acid. [Archaic.] Oxymuriatic acid, chlorine,
formerly so called on the supposition that it was a compound of
oxygen and muriatic acid. [Obs.]
