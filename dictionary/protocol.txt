PROTOCOL
Pro"to*col, n. Etym: [F. protocole, LL. protocollum, fr. Gr. prw	os
the first (see Proto-) +

1. The original copy of any writing, as of a deed, treaty, dispatch,
or other instrument. Burrill.

2. The minutes, or rough draught, of an instrument or transaction.

3. (Diplomacy)
(a) A preliminary document upon the basis of which negotiations are
carried on.
(b) A convention not formally ratified.
(c) An agreement of diplomatists indicating the results reached by
them at a particular stage of a negotiation.
PROTOCOL

=====
Pro"to*col, v. t.

Defn: To make a protocol of.
PROTOCOL

=====
Pro"to*col, v. i.

Defn: To make or write protocols, or first draughts; to issue
protocols. Carlyle.
