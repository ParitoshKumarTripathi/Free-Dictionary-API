UNSEASON
Un*sea"son, v. t. Etym: [1st pref. un- + season.]

1. To make unseasoned; to deprive of seasoning.

2. To strike unseasonably; to affect disagreeably or unfavorably.
[Obs.]
Why do I send this rustic madrigal, That may thy tuneful ear unseason
quite Spenser.
