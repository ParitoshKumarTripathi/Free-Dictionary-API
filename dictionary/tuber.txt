TUBER
Tu"ber, n.Etym: [L., a hump. knob; probably akin to tumere to swell.
Cf. Tumid.]

1. (Bot.)
(a) A fleshy, rounded stem or root, usually containing starchy
matter, as the potato or arrowroot; a thickened root-stock. See
Illust. of Tuberous.
(b) A genus of fungi. See Truffle.

2. (Anat.)

Defn: A tuberosity; a tubercle.
