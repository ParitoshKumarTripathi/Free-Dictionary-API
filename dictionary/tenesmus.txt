TENESMUS
Te*nes"mus, n. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. tenesmos.] (Med.)

Defn: An urgent and distressing sensation, as if a discharge from the
intestines must take place, although none can be effected; -- always
referred to the lower extremity of the rectum. Vesical tenesmus, a
similar sensation as to the evacuation of urine, referred to the
region of the bladder.
