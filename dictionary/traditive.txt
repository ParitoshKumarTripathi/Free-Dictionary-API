TRADITIVE
Trad"i*tive, a. Etym: [L. tradere, traditum, to transmit, give up:
cf. F. traditif.]

Defn: Transmitted or transmissible from father to son, or from age,
by oral communication; traditional. [R.] Jer. Taylor.
Suppose we on things traditive divide. Dryden.
