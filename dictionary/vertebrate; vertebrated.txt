VERTEBRATE; VERTEBRATED
Ver"te*brate, Ver"te*bra`ted, a. Etym: [L. vertebratus.]

1. (Anat.)

Defn: Having a backbone, or vertebral column, containing the spinal
marrow, as man, quadrupeds, birds, amphibia, and fishes.

2. (Bot.)

Defn: Contracted at intervals, so as to resemble the spine in
animals. Henslow.

3. (Zoöl.)

Defn: Having movable joints resembling vertebræ; -- said of the arms
ophiurans.

4. (Zoöl.)

Defn: Of or pertaining to the Vertebrata; -- used only in the form
vertebrate.
