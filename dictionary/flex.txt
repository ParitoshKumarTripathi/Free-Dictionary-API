FLEX
Flex, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Flexed; p. pr. & vb. n. Flexing.] Etym: [L.
flexus, p.p. of flectere to bend, perh. flectere and akin to falx
sickle, E. falchion. Cf. Flinch.]

Defn: To bend; as, to flex the arm.
FLEX

=====
Flex, n.

Defn: Flax. [Obs.] Chaucer.
