OBITER
Ob"i*ter, adv. Etym: [L., on the way; ob (see Ob-) + iter a going, a
walk, way.]

Defn: In passing; incidentally; by the way. Obiter dictum (Law), an
incidental and collateral opinion uttered by a judge. See Dictum, n.,
2(a).
