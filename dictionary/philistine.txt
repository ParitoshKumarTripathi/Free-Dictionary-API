PHILISTINE
Phi*lis"tine, n. Etym: [L. Philistinus, Heb. Phlishthi, pl.
Phlishthim.]

1. A native or an inhabitant of ancient Philistia, a coast region of
southern Palestine.

2. A bailiff. [Cant, Eng.] [Obs.] Swift.

3. A person deficient in liberal culture and refinement; one without
appreciation of the nobler aspirations and sentiments of humanity;
one whose scope is limited to selfish and material interests.
[Recent] M. Arnold.
PHILISTINE

=====
Phi*lis"tine, a.

1. Of or pertaining to the Philistines.

2. Uncultured; commonplace.
