FOREMOST
Fore"most`, a. Etym: [OE. formest first, AS. formest, fyrmest,
superl. of forma first, which is a superl. fr. fore fore; cf. Goth.
frumist, fruma, first. See Fore, adv., and cf. First, Former, Frame,
v. t., Prime, a.]

Defn: First in time or place; most advanced; chief in rank or
dignity; as, the foremost troops of an army.
THat struck the foremost man of all this world. Shak.
