PALL-MALL
Pall`-mall", n. Etym: [OF. palemail, It. pallamagio; palla a ball (of
German origin, akin to E. ball) + magio hammer, fr. L. malleus. See
lst Ball, and Mall a beetle.]

Defn: A game formerly common in England, in which a wooden ball was
driven with a mallet through an elevated hoop or ring of iron. The
name was also given to the mallet used, to the place where the game
was played, and to the street, in London, still called Pall Mall.
[Written also pail-mail and pell-mell.] Sir K. Digby. Evelyn.
