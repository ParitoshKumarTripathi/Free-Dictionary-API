RAMAGE
Ram"age (; 48), n. Etym: [F., fr. L. ramus a branch.]

1. Boughs or branches. [Obs.] Crabb.

2. Warbling of birds in trees. [Obs.] Drummond.
RAMAGE

=====
Ra*mage", a.

Defn: Wild; untamed. [Obs.]
