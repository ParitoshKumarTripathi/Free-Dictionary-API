URUS
U"rus, n. Etym: [L.; of Teutonic origin. See Aurochs.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: A very large, powerful, and savage extinct bovine animal (Bos
urus or primigenius) anciently abundant in Europe. It appears to have
still existed in the time of Julius Cæsar. It had very large horns,
and was hardly capable of domestication. Called also, ur, ure, and
tur.
