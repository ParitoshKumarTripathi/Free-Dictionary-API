SCAPHOPODA
Sca*phop"o*da, n. pl. Etym: [NL., from Gr. -poda.] (Zo√∂l.)

Defn: A class of marine cephalate Mollusca having a tubular shell
open at both ends, a pointed or spadelike foot for burrowing, and
many long, slender, prehensile oral tentacles. It includes Dentalium,
or the tooth shells, and other similar shells. Called also
Prosopocephala, and Solenoconcha.
