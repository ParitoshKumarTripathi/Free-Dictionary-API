RHUMB
Rhumb, n. Etym: [F. rumb, Sp. rumbo, or Pg. rumbo, rumo, probably fr.
Gr. Rhomb.] (Navigation)

Defn: A line which crosses successive meridians at a constant angle;
-- called also rhumb line, and loxodromic curve. See Loxodromic. To
sail on a rhumb, to sail continuously on one course, following a
rhumb line.
