TRALATITIOUS
Tral`a*ti"tious, a. Etym: [L. tralatitius, translatitius,
tralaticius, translaticius. See Tralation.]

1. Passed along; handed down; transmitted.
Among biblical critics a tralatitious interpretation is one received
by expositor from expositor. W. Withington.

2. Metaphorical; figurative; not literal. Stackhouse.
