SABBATARIAN
Sab`ba*ta"ri*an, n. Etym: [L. Sabbatarius: cf. F. sabbataire. See
Sabbath.]

1. One who regards and keeps the seventh day of the week as holy,
aggreeably to the letter of the fourth commandment in the Decalogue.

Note: There were Christians in the early church who held this
opinion, and certain Christians, esp. the Seventh-day Baptists, hold
it now.

2. A strict observer of the Sabbath.
SABBATARIAN

=====
Sab`ba*ta"ri*an, a.

Defn: Of or pertaining to the Sabbath, or the tenets of Sabbatarians.
