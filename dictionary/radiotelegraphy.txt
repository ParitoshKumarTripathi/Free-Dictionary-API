RADIOTELEGRAPHY
Ra`di*o*te*leg"ra*phy, n. [Radio- + telegraphy.]

Defn: Telegraphy using the radiant energy of electrical (Hertzian)
waves; wireless telegraphy; -- the term adopted for use by the
Radiotelegraphic Convention of 1912.
