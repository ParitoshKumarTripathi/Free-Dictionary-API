HIERARCH
Hi"er*arch, n. Etym: [LL. hierarcha, Gr. ishiras vigorous, fresh,
blooming) + hiérarque.]

Defn: One who has high and controlling authority in sacred things;
the chief of a sacred order; as, princely hierarchs. Milton.
