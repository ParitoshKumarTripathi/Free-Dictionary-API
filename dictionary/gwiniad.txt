GWINIAD
Gwin"i*ad, n. Etym: [W. gwyniad a whiting, the name of various
fishes, fr. gwyn white.] (Zool.)

Defn: A fish (Coregonus ferus) of North Wales and Northern Europe,
allied to the lake whitefish; -- called also powan, and schelly.
[Written also gwyniad, guiniad, gurniad.]
