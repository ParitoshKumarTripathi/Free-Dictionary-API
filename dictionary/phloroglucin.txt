PHLOROGLUCIN
Phlor`o*glu"cin, n. Etym: [Phloretin + Gr. (Chem.)

Defn: A sweet white crystalline substance, metameric with pyrogallol,
and obtained by the decomposition of phloretin, and from certain
gums, as catechu, kino, etc. It belongs to the class of phenols.
[Called also phloroglucinol.]
