VERTIGO
Ver"ti*go, n; pl. E. Vertigoes, L. Vertigines. Etym: [L., fr. vertere
to turn. See Verse.]

1. (Med.)

Defn: Dizziness or swimming of the head; an affection of the head in
which objects, though stationary, appear to move in various
directions, and the person affected finds it difficult to maintain an
erect posture; giddiness. Quian.

2. (Zoöl.)

Defn: Any one of numerous species of small land snails belonging to
the genus Vertigo, having an elongated or conical spiral shell and
usually teeth in the aperture.
