SKITTER
Skit"ter, v. t. [Cf. Skit, v. t.]

Defn: To move or pass (something) over a surface quickly so that it
touches only at intervals; to skip.

The angler, standing in the bow, 'skitters' or skips the spoon over
the surface.
James A. Henshall.
SKITTER

=====
Skit"ter, v. i.

Defn: To pass or glide lightly or with quick touches at intervals; to
skip; to skim.

Some kinds of ducks in lighting strike the water with their tails
first, and skitter along the surface for a feet before settling down.
T. Roosevelt.
