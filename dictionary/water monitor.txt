WATER MONITOR
Wa"ter mon"i*tor. (Zoöl.)

Defn: A very large lizard (Varanaus salvator) native of India. It
frequents the borders of streams and swims actively. It becomes five
or six feet long. Called also two-banded monitor, and kabaragoya. The
name is also applied to other aquatic monitors.
