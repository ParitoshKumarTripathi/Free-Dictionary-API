PYROPHOSPHORIC
Pyr`o*phos*phor"ic, a. Etym: [Pyro- + phosphoric.] (Chem.)

Defn: Pertaining to, or designating, an acid, H4P2O7, which is
obtained as a white crystalline substance. Its salts are obtained by
heating the phosphates.
