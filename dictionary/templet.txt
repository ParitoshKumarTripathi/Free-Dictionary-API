TEMPLET
Tem"plet, n. Etym: [LL. templatus vaulted, from L. templum a small
timber.] [Spelt also template.]

1. A gauge, pattern, or mold, commonly a thin plate or board, used as
a guide to the form of the work to be executed; as, a mason's or a
wheelwright's templet.

2. (Arch.)

Defn: A short piece of timber, iron, or stone, placed in a wall under
a girder or other beam, to distribute the weight or pressure.
