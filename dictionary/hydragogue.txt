HYDRAGOGUE
Hy"dra*gogue, a. Etym: [L. hydragogus conveying off water, Gr. "y`dwr
water + hydragogue.] (Med.)

Defn: Causing a discharge of water; expelling serum effused into any
part of the body, as in dropsy.
 -- n.

Defn: A hydragogue medicine, usually a cathartic or diuretic.
