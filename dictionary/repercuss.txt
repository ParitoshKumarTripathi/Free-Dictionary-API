REPERCUSS
Re`per*cuss" (-ks"), v. t. [imp. & p. p. Repercussed (-kst");p. pr. &
vb. n. Repercussing.] Etym: [L. repercusus, p. p. of repercutere to
drive back; pref. re- re- + percutere. See Percussion.]

Defn: To drive or beat back; hence, to reflect; to reverberate.
Perceiving all the subjacent country, . . . to repercuss such a light
as I could hardly look against. Evelyn.
