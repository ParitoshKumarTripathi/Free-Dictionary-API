SIDING
Sid"ing, n.

1. Attaching one's self to a party.

2. A side track, as a railroad; a turnout.

3. (Carp.)

Defn: The covering of the outside wall of a frame house, whether made
of weatherboards, vertical boarding with cleats, shingles, or the
like.

4. (Shipbuilding)

Defn: The thickness of a rib or timber, measured, at right angles
with its side, across the curved edge; as, a timber having a siding
of ten inches.
