VOCULE
Voc"ule, n. Etym: [L. vocula, dim. of vox, vocis, voice.] (Phon.)

Defn: A short or weak utterance; a faint or feeble sound, as that
heard on separating the lips in pronouncing p or b. Rush.
 -- Voc"u*lar, a.
