SETON
Se"ton, n. Etym: [F. séton (cf. It. setone), from L. seta a thick,
stiff hair, a bristle.] (Med. & Far.)

Defn: A few silk threads or horsehairs, or a strip of linen or the
like, introduced beneath the skin by a knife or needle, so as to form
an issue; also, the issue so formed.
