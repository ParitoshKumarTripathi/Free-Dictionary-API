VELITATION
Vel`i*ta"tion, n. Etym: [L. velitatio, fr. velitari, velitatus, to
skirmish, from veles, -itis, a light-armed soldier.]

Defn: A dispute or contest; a slight contest; a skirmish. [R.] Sir M.
Hale.
After a short velitation we parted. Evelyn.
