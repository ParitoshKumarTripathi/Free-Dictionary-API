TROCHANTER
Tro*chan"ter, n. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr.

1. (Anat.)

Defn: One of two processes near the head of the femur, the outer
being called the great trochanter, and the inner the small
trochanter.

2. (Zoöl.)

Defn: The third joint of the leg of an insect, or the second when the
trochantine is united with the coxa.
