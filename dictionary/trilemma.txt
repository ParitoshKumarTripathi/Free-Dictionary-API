TRILEMMA
Tri*lem"ma, n. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. Tri-) + any thing received, in
logic, an assumption. Cf. Dilemma.]

1. (Logic)

Defn: A syllogism with three conditional propositions, the major
premises of which are disjunctively affirmed in the minor. See
Dilemma.

2. A state of things in which it is difficult to determine which one
of three courses to pursue.
