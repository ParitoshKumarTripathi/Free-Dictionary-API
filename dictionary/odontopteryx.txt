ODONTOPTERYX
O`don*top"te*ryx, n. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. 'odoy`s, 'odo`ntos, a tooth
+ pte`ryx a wing.] (Paleon.)

Defn: An extinct Eocene bird having the jaws strongly serrated, or
dentated, but destitute of true teeth. It was found near London.
