VELL
Vell, n. Etym: [Cf. L. vellus the skin of a sheep with the wool on
it, a fleece, a hide or pelt, or E. fell a hide.]

Defn: The salted stomach of a calf, used in making cheese; a rennet
bag. [Prov. Eng.]
VELL

=====
Vell, v. i. Etym: [Cf. Vell, n.]

Defn: To cut the turf from, as for burning. [Prov. Eng.] Halliwell.
