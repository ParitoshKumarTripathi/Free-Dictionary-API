HYDRAMINE
Hy*dram"ine, n. Etym: [Hydroxyl + amine.] (Chem.)

Defn: One of a series of artificial, organic bases, usually produced
as thick viscous liquids by the action of ammonia on ethylene oxide.
They have the properties both of alcohol and amines.
