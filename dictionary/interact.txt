INTERACT
In`ter*act", n. Etym: [Pref. inter- + act. Cf. Entr'acte.]

Defn: A short act or piece between others, as in a play; an
interlude; hence, intermediate employment or time. Chesterfield.
INTERACT

=====
In`ter*act", v. i.

Defn: To act upon each other; as, two agents mutually interact.
Emerson. Tyndall.
