TERGIVERSATE
Ter"gi*ver*sate, v. i. Etym: [L. tergiversatus, p.p. of tergiversari
to turn one's back, to shift; tergum back + versare, freq. of vertere
to turn. See Verse.]

Defn: To shift; to practice evasion; to use subterfuges; to shuffle.
[R.] Bailey.
