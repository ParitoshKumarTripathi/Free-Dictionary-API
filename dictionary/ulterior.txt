ULTERIOR
Ul*te"ri*or, a. Etym: [L., comp. of ultra, ultro, beyond, on the
other side, properly cases of an old adjective, formed with a
comparative suffix, which is akin to OL. uls beyond, L. olim
formerly, hereafter, orig., at that time, ille that, OL. olle, ollus.
Cf. Outrage.]

1. Situated beyond, or on the farther side; thither; -- correlative
with hither.

2. Further; remoter; more distant; succeeding; as, ulterior demands
or propositions; ulterior views; what ulterior measures will be
adopted is uncertain. Ulterior object or aim, an object or aim beyond
that which is avowed.
ULTERIOR

=====
Ul*te"ri*or, n.

Defn: Ulterior side or part. [R.] Coleridge.
