TIRAILLEUR
Ti`rail`leur", n. Etym: [F., from tirailler to skirmish, wrest, from
tirer to draw.] (Mil.)

Defn: Formerly, a member of an independent body of marksmen in the
French army. They were used sometimes in front of the army to annoy
the enemy, sometimes in the rear to check his pursuit. The term is
now applied to all troops acting as skirmishers.
