TRIPHTHONG
Triph"thong, n. Etym: [Pref. tri- + -phthong, as in diphthong: cf. F.
triphthonque.] (OrthoÃ«py)

Defn: A combination of three vowel sounds in a single syllable,
forming a simple or compound sound; also, a union of three vowel
characters, representing together a single sound; a trigraph; as,
eye, -ieu in adieu, -eau in beau, are examples of triphthongs.
