DWANG
Dwang, n. Etym: [Cf. D. dwingen to force, compel.]

1. (Carp.)

Defn: A piece of wood set between two studs, posts, etc., to stiffen
and support them.

2. (Mech.)
(a) A kind of crowbar.
(b) A large wrench. Knight.
