GESTICULATE
Ges*tic"u*late, v. i. [imp. & p. p. Gesticulated; p. pr. & vb. n.
Gesticulating.] Etym: [L. gesticulatus, p. p. of gesticulari to
gesticulate, fr. gesticulus a mimic gesture, gesticulation, dim. of
gestus gesture, fr. gerere, gestum, to bear, carry, peform. See
Gestic.]

Defn: To make gestures or motions, as in speaking; to use postures.
Sir T. Herbert.
GESTICULATE

=====
Ges*tic"u*late, v. t.

Defn: To represent by gesture; to act. [R.] B. Jonson.
