TETRAMETER
Te*tram"e*ter, n. Etym: [L. tetrametrus, Gr. Tetra-) + tétramètre.]
(GR. & Latin Pros.)

Defn: A verse or line consisting of four measures, that is, in
iambic, trochaic, and anapestic verse, of eight feet; in other kinds
of verse, of four feet.
