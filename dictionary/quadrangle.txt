QUADRANGLE
Quad"ran`gle, n. Etym: [F., fr. L. quadrangulum; quattuor four +
angulus an angle. See Four, and Angle a corner.]

1. (Geom.)

Defn: A plane figure having four angles, and consequently four sides;
any figure having four angles.

2. A square or quadrangular space or inclosure, such a space or court
surrounded by buildings, esp. such a court in a college or public
school in England.
