WAGGERY
Wag"ger*y, n.; pl. Waggeries. Etym: [From Wag.]

Defn: The manner or action of a wag; mischievous merriment; sportive
trick or gayety; good-humored sarcasm; pleasantry; jocularity; as,
the waggery of a schoolboy. Locke.
A drollery and lurking waggery of expression. W. Irving.
