THOTH
Thoth, n.

1. (Myth.)

Defn: The god of eloquence and letters among the ancient Egyptians,
and supposed to be the inventor of writing and philosophy. He
corresponded to the Mercury of the Romans, and was usually
represented as a human figure with the head of an ibis or a lamb.
