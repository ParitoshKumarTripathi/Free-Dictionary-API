PYROSOME
Pyr"o*some, n. Etym: [Pyro- + -some body.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: Any compound ascidian of the genus Pyrosoma. The pyrosomes form
large hollow cylinders, sometimes two or three feet long, which swim
at the surface of the sea and are very phosphorescent.
