SILICULOSE
Si*lic"u*lose`, a. Etym: [NL. siliculosus, fr. L. silicula: cf. F.
siliculeux. See Silicle.]

1. (Bot.)

Defn: Bearing silicles; pertaining to, or resembling, silicles.

2. Full of, or consisting of, husks; husky. [Obs.]
