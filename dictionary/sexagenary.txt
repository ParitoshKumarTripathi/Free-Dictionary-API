SEXAGENARY
Sex*ag"e*na*ry, a. Etym: [L. sexagenarius, fr. sexageni sixty each,
akin to sexaginta sixty, sex six: cf. sexagénaire. See Six.]

Defn: Pertaining to, or designating, the number sixty; poceeding by
sixties; sixty years old. Sexagenary arithmetic. See under
Sexagesimal.
 -- Sexagenary, or Sexagesimal, scale (Math.), a scale of numbers in
which the modulus is sixty. It is used in treating the divisions of
the circle.
SEXAGENARY

=====
Sex*ag"e*na*ry, n.

1. Something composed of sixty parts or divisions.

2. A sexagenarian. Sir W. Scott.
