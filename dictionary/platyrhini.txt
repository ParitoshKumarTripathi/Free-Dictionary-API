PLATYRHINI
Plat`y*rhi"ni, n. pl. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. (Zoöl.)

Defn: A division of monkeys, including the American species, which
have a broad nasal septum, thirty-six teeth, and usually a prehensile
tail. See Monkey. [Written also Platyrrhini.]
