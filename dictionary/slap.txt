SLAP
Slap, n. Etym: [OE. slappe; akin to LG. slappe, G. schlappe; probably
of imitative origin.]

Defn: A blow, esp. one given with the open hand, or with something
broad.
SLAP

=====
Slap, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Slapped; p. pr. & vb. n. Slapping.]

Defn: To strike with the open hand, or with something broad.
SLAP

=====
Slap, adv. Etym: [Cf. LG. slap, G. schlapp. See Slap, n.]

Defn: With a sudden and violent blow; hence, quickly; instantly;
directly. [Colloq.] "The railroad cars drive slap into the city."
Thackeray.
