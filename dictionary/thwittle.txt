THWITTLE
Thwit"tle, v. t. Etym: [See Thwite, and Whittle.]

Defn: To cut or whittle. [Obs. or Prov. Eng.] Palsgrave.
THWITTLE

=====
Thwit"tle, n.

Defn: A small knife; a whittle. [Written also thwitel.] [Obs.] "A
Sheffield thwittle." Chaucer.
