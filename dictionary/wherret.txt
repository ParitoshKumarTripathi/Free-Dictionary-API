WHERRET
Wher"ret, v. t. Etym: [From Whir.]

1. To hurry; to trouble; to tease. [Obs.] Bickerstaff.

2. To box (one) on the ear; to strike or box. (the ear); as, to
wherret a child. [Obs.]
WHERRET

=====
Wher"ret, n.

Defn: A box on the ear. [Obs.] Beau. & Fl.
