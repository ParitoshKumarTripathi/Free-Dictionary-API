VIRTUOSO
Vir`tu*o"so, n.; pl. Virtuosos; It. Virtuosi. Etym: [It. See
Virtuous.]

1. One devoted to virtu; one skilled in the fine arts, in
antiquities, and the like; a collector or ardent admirer of
curiosities, etc.
Virtuoso the Italians call a man who loves the noble arts, and is a
critic in them. Dryden.

2. (Mus.)

Defn: A performer on some instrument, as the violin or the piano, who
excels in the technical part of his art; a brilliant concert player.
