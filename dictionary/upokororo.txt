UPOKORORO
U`po*ko*ro"ro, n. Etym: [From the native Maori name.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: An edible fresh-water New Zealand fish (Prototroctes
oxyrhynchus) of the family Haplochitonidæ. In general appearance and
habits, it resembles the northern lake whitefishes and trout. Called
also grayling.
