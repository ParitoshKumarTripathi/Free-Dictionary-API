TALIPOT
Tal"i*pot, n. Etym: [Hind. talpat the leaf of the tree.] (Bot.)

Defn: A beautiful tropical palm tree (Corypha umbraculifera), a
native of Ceylon and the Malabar coast. It has a trunk sixty or
seventy feet high, bearing a crown of gigantic fan-shaped leaves
which are used as umbrellas and as fans in ceremonial processions,
and, when cut into strips, as a substitute for writing paper.
