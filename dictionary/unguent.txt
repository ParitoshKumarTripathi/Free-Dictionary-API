UNGUENT
Un"guent, n. Etym: [L. unguentum, from unguere, ungere, to anoint:
cf. F. onguent. See Ointment, and cf. Unction, Unctuous.]

Defn: A lubricant or salve for sores, burns, or the like; an
ointment. Cowper.

Note: An unguent is stiffer than a liniment, but softer than a
cerate.
