WATER THERMOMETER
Wa"ter ther*mom"e*ter. (Physics)

Defn: A thermometer filled with water instead of mercury, for
ascertaining the precise temperature at which water attains its
maximum density. This is about 39º Fahr., or 4º Centigrade; and from
that point down to 32º Fahr., or 0º Centigrade, or the freezing
point, it expands.
