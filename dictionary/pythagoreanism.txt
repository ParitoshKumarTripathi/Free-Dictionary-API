PYTHAGOREANISM
Pyth`a*go"re*an*ism, n.

Defn: The doctrines of Pythagoras or the Pythagoreans.
As a philosophic school Pythagoreanism became extinct in Greece about
the middle of the 4th century [B. C.]. Encyc. Brit.
