WHALA
Whala, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Whaled; p. pr. & vb. n. Whaling.] Etym:
[Cf. Wale. ]

Defn: To lash with stripes; to wale; to thrash; to drub. [Prov. Eng.
& Colloq. U. S.] Halliwell. Bartlett.
