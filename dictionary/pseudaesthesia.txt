PSEUDAESTHESIA
Pseu`dæs*the"si*a, n. Etym: [NL. See Pseudo-, and Æsthesia.]
(Physiol.)

Defn: False or imaginary feeling or sense perception such as occurs
in hypochondriasis, or such as is referred to an organ that has been
removed, as an amputated foot.
