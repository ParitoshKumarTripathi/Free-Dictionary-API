UROHAEMATIN
U`ro*hæm"a*tin, n. Etym: [1st uro- + hæmatin.] (Physiol. Chem.)

Defn: Urinary hæmatin; -- applied to the normal coloring matter of
the urine, on the supposition that it is formed either directly or
indirectly (through bilirubin) from the hæmatin of the blood. See
Urochrome, and Urobilin.
