VOCABLE
Vo"ca*ble, n. Etym: [L. vocabulum an appellation, designation, name,
fr. vocare to call, fr. vox, vocis, a voice, a word: cf. F. vocable.
See Voice.]

Defn: A word; a term; a name; specifically, a word considered as
composed of certain sounds or letters, without regard to its meaning.
Swamped near to drowning in a tide of ingenious vocables. Carlyle.
