PEDAGOGUE
Ped"a*gogue, n. Etym: [F. p√©dagogue, L. paedagogus, Gr. Page a
servant, Agent.]

1. (Gr. Antiq.)

Defn: A slave who led his master's children to school, and had the
charge of them generally.

2. A teacher of children; one whose occupation is to teach the young;
a schoolmaster.

3. One who by teaching has become formal, positive, or pedantic in
his ways; one who has the manner of a schoolmaster; a pedant.
Goldsmith.
PEDAGOGUE

=====
Ped"a*gogue, v. t. Etym: [Cf. L. paedagogare to instruct.]

Defn: To play the pedagogue toward. [Obs.] Prior.
