OGEE
O*gee", n. Etym: [F. ogive, augive, LL. augiva, of uncertain origin;
cf.LL. ogis a support, prop. L. augere to increase, strengthen, Sp.
auge highest point of power or fortune, apogee, Ar. auj, an
astronomical term.]

1. (Arch.)

Defn: A molding, the section of which is the form of the letter S,
with the convex part above; cyma reversa. See Illust. under Cyma.

2. Hence, any similar figure used for any purpose. Ogee arch (Arch.),
a pointed arch, each of the sides of which has the curve of an ogee,
that is, has a reversed curve near the apex.
