PALADIN
Pal"a*din, n. Etym: [F., fr.It. paladino, fr. L. palatinus an officer
of the palace. See Palatine.]

Defn: A knight-errant; a distinguished champion; as, the paladins of
Charlemagne. Sir W. Scott.
