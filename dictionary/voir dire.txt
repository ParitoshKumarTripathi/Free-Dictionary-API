VOIR DIRE
Voir dire. Etym: [OF., to say the truth, fr. L. verus true + dicere
to say.] (Law)

Defn: An oath administered to a witness, usually before being sworn
in chief, requiring him to speak the truth, or make true answers in
reference to matters inquired of, to ascertain his competency to give
evidence. Greenleaf. Ld. Abinger.
