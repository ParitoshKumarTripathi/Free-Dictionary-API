STRANGULATION
Stran"gu*la`tion, n. Etym: [L. strangulatio: cf. F. strangulation.
See Strangle.]

1. The act of strangling, or the state of being strangled.

2. (Med.)

Defn: Inordinate compression or constriction of a tube or part, as of
the throat; especially, such as causes a suspension of breathing, of
the passage of contents, or of the circulation, as in cases of
hernia.
