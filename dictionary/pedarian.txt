PEDARIAN
Pe*da"ri*an, n. Etym: [L. pedarius, fr. pedarius belonging to the
foot, fr. pes, pedis, foot.] (Rom. Antiq.)

Defn: One of a class eligible to the office of senator, but not yet
chosen, who could sit and speak in the senate, but could not vote; --
so called because he might indicate his opinion by walking over to
the side of the party he favored when a vote was taken.
