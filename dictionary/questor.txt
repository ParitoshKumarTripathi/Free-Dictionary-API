QUESTOR
Ques"tor, n. Etym: [L. quaestor, contr. fr. quaesitor, fr. quaerere,
quaesitum, to seek for, ask: cf. F. questeur.] (Rom. Antiq.)

Defn: An officer who had the management of the public treasure; a
receiver of taxes, tribute, etc.; treasurer of state. [Written also
quæstor.]

Note: At an early period there were also public accusers styled
questors, but the office was soon abolished.
