RESPIRATOR
Res"pi*ra`tor (rs"p*r`tr), n. Etym: [Cf. F. respirateur.]

Defn: A divice of gauze or wire, covering the mouth or nose, to
prevent the inhalation of noxious substances, as dust or smoke. Being
warmed by the breath, it tempers cold air passing through it, and may
also be used for the inhalation of medicated vapors.
