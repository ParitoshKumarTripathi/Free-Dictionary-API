PHOTOGEN
Pho"to*gen, n. Etym: [Photo- + -gen.] (Chem.)

Defn: A light hydrocarbon oil resembling kerosene. It is obtained by
distilling coal, paraffin, etc., and is used as a lubricant,
illuminant, etc. [Written also photogene.]
