OXANILAMIDE; OXANILLAMIDE
Ox`a*nil*am"ide, Ox`a*nill*am"ide, n. Etym: [Oxanilic + amide.]
(Chem.)

Defn: A white crystalline nitrogenous substance, obtained indirectly
by the action of cyanogen on aniline, and regarded as an anilide of
oxamic acid; -- called also phenyl oxamide.
