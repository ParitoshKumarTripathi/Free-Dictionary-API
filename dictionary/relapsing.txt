RELAPSING
Re*laps"ing, a.

Defn: Marked by a relapse; falling back; tending to return to a
former worse state. Relapsing fever (Med.), an acute, epidemic,
contagious fever, which prevails also endemically in Ireland, Russia,
and some other regions. It is marked by one or two remissions of the
fever, by articular and muscular pains, and by the presence, during
the paroxism of spiral bacterium (Spirochæte) in the blood. It is not
usually fatal. Called also famine fever, and recurring fever.
