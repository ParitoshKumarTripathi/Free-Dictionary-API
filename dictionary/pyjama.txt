PYJAMA
Py*ja"ma, n. Etym: [Hind. pae-jama, literally, leg clothing.]

Defn: In India and Persia, thin loose trowsers or drawers; in Europe
and America, drawers worn at night, or a kind of nightdress with
legs. [Written also paijama.]
