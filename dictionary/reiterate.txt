REITERATE
Re*it"er*ate (-at), v. t. [imp. & p. p. Reiterated (-a`têd); p. pr. &
vb. n. Reiterating.] Etym: [Pref. re- + iterate: cf. F. réitérer, LL.
reiterare to question again.]

Defn: To repeat again and again; to say or do repeatedly; sometimes,
to repeat.
That with reiterated crimes he might Heap on himself damnation.
Milton.
You never spoke what did become you less Than this; which to
reiterate were sin. Shak.

Syn.
 -- To repeat; recapitulate; rehearse.
REITERATE

=====
Re*it"er*ate (-t), a.

Defn: Reiterated; repeated. [R.]
