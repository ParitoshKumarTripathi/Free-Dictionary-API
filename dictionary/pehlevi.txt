PEHLEVI
Peh"le*vi`, n. Etym: [Parsee Pahlavi.]

Defn: An ancient Persian dialect in which words were partly
represented by their Semitic equivalents. It was in use from the 3d
century (and perhaps earlier) to the middle of the 7th century, and
later in religious writings. [Written also Pahlavi.]
