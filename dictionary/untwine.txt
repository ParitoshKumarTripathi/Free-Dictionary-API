UNTWINE
Un*twine", v. t. Etym: [1st pref. un- + twine.]

Defn: To untwist; to separate, as that which is twined or twisted; to
disentangle; to untie.
It requires a long and powerful counter sympathy in a nation to
untwine the ties of custom which bind a people to the established and
the old. Sir W. Hamilton.
UNTWINE

=====
Un*twine", v. i.

Defn: To become untwined. Milton.
