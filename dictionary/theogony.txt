THEOGONY
The*og"o*ny, n. Etym: [L. theogonia, Gr. Theism, and Genus.]

Defn: The generation or genealogy of the gods; that branch of heathen
theology which deals with the origin and descent of the deities;
also, a poem treating of such genealogies; as, the Theogony of
Hesiod.
