HETEROTOPISM; HETEROTOPY
Het`er*ot"o*pism, Het`er*ot"o*py, n. Etym: [Hetero- + Gr.
hétérotopie.]

1. (Med.)

Defn: A deviation from the natural position; -- a term applied in the
case of organs or growths which are abnormal in situation.

2. (Biol.)

Defn: A deviation from the natural position of parts, supposed to be
effected in thousands of years, by the gradual displacement of germ
cells.
