TOUSE; TOUZE
Touse, Touze, v. t. & i. [imp. & p. p. Toused; p. pr. & vb. n.
Tousing.] Etym: [OE. tosen sq. root64. See tease, and cf. Tose, Toze.
]

Defn: To pull; to haul; to tear; to worry. [Prov. Eng.] Shak.
As a bear, whom angry curs have touzed. Spenser.
