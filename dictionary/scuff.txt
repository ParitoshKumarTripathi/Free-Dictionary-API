SCUFF
Scuff, n. Etym: [Cf. D. schoft shoulder, Goth. skuft hair of the
head. Cf. Scruff.]

Defn: The back part of the neck; the scruff. [Prov. Eng.] Ld. Lytton.
SCUFF

=====
Scuff, v. i. [imp. & p. p. Scuffed; p. pr. & vb. n. Scuffing.] Etym:
[See Scuffle.]

Defn: To walk without lifting the feet; to proceed with a scraping or
dragging movement; to shuffle.
