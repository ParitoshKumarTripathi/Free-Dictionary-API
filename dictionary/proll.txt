PROLL
Proll, v. t. Etym: [See Prowl.] [imp. & p. p. Prolled (; p. pr. & vb.
n. Prolling.]

Defn: To search or prowl after; to rob; to plunder. [Obs.] Barrow.
PROLL

=====
Proll, v. i.

Defn: To prowl about; to rob. [Obs.] South.
Though ye prolle aye, ye shall it never find. Chaucer.
