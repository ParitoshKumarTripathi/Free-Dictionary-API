UNIVERSE
U"ni*verse, n. Etym: [L. universum, from universus universal; unus
one + vertere, versum, to turn, that is, turned into one, combined
into one whole; cf. F. univers. See One, and Verse.]

Defn: All created things viewed as constituting one system or whole;
the whole body of things, or of phenomena; the mundus of the Latins;
the world; creation.
How may I Adore thee, Author of this universe And all this good to
man! Milton.
