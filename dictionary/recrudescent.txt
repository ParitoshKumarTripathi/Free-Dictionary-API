RECRUDESCENT
Re`cru*des"cent (-sent), a. Etym: [L. recrudescens, -entis, p.pr. of
recrudescere to become raw again; pref. re- re- + crudescere to
become hard or raw: cf. F. recrudescent.]

1. Growing raw, sore, or painful again.

2. Breaking out again after temporary abatement or supression; as, a
recrudescent epidemic.
