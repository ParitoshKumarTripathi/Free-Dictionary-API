OUTWIT
Out*wit", v. t.

Defn: To surpass in wisdom, esp. in cunning; to defeat or overreach
by superior craft.
They did so much outwit and outwealth us ! Gauden.
OUTWIT

=====
Out"wit, n.

Defn: The faculty of acquiring wesdom by observation and experience,
or the wisdom so acquired; -- opposed to inwit. [Obs.] Piers Plowman.
