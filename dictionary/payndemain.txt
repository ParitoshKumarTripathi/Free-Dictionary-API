PAYNDEMAIN
Payn`de*main", n. Etym: [OF. pain bread + demaine manorial, lordly,
own, private. See Payn, and Demesne. Said to be so called from the
figure of our Lord impressed upon it.]

Defn: The finest and whitest bread made in the Middle Ages; -- called
also paynemain, payman. [Obs.]
