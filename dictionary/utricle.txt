UTRICLE
U"tri*cle, n. Etym: [L. utriculus a little womb, a calycle, dim. of
uter, utris, a bag or bottle made of an animal's hide: cf. F.
utricule.]

1. A little sac or vesicle, as the air cell of fucus, or seaweed.

2. (Physiol.)

Defn: A microscopic cell in the structure of an egg, animal, or
plant.

3. (Bot.)

Defn: A small, thin-walled, one-seeded fruit, as of goosefoot. Gray.

4. (Anat.)

Defn: A utriculus.
