PULLET
Pul"let, n. Etym: [OE. polete, OF. polete, F. poulette, dim. of poule
a hen, fr. L. pullus a young animal, a young fowl. See Foal, and cf.
Poult, Poultry, Pool stake.]

Defn: A young hen, or female of the domestic fowl. Pullet sperm, the
treadle of an egg. [Obs.] Shak.
