OXALANTIN
Ox`a*lan"tin, n. Etym: [From Alloxantin, by transposition of
letters.] (Chem.)

Defn: A white crystalline nitrogenous substance (C6H4N4O5) obtained
by the reduction of parabanic acid; -- called also leucoturic acid.
