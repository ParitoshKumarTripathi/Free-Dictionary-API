TRAPEZOID
Trap"e*zoid, n. Etym: [Gr. trapézoïde. See Trapezium.]

1. (Geom.)

Defn: A plane four-sided figure, having two sides parallel to each
other.

2. (Anat.)

Defn: A bone of the carpus at the base of the second metacarpal, or
index finger.
TRAPEZOID

=====
Trap"e*zoid, a.

1. Having the form of a trapezoid; trapezoidal; as, the trapezoid
ligament which connects the coracoid process and the clavicle.

2. (Anat.)

Defn: Of or pertaining to the trapezoid ligament; as, the trapezoid
line.
