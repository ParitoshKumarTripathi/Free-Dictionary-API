SYNCHYSIS
Syn"chy*sis, n. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr.

Defn: A derangement or confusion of any kind, as of words in a
sentence, or of humors in the eye. Sparkling synchysis (Med.), a
condition in which the vitreous humor is softened and contains
sparkling scales of cholesterin.
