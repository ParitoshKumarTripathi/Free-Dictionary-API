STRANGURY
Stran"gu*ry, n. Etym: [L. stranguria, Gr. strangurie. See Strangle,
and Urine.]

1. (Med.)

Defn: A painful discharge of urine, drop by drop, produced by
spasmodic muscular contraction.

2. (Bot.)

Defn: A swelling or other disease in a plant, occasioned by a
ligature fastened tightly about it.
