HEELPATH
Heel"path`, n. [So called with a play upon the words tow and toe.]

Defn: The bank of a canal opposite, and corresponding to, that of the
towpath; berm. [U. S.]

The Cowles found convenient spiles sunk in the heelpath.
The Century.
