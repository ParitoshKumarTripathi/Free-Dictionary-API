TRANSILIENCE; TRANSILIENCY
Tran*sil"i*ence, Tran*sil"i*en*cy, n. Etym: [L. transiliens, p. pr.
of transilire to leap across or over; trans across, over + salire to
leap.]

Defn: A leap across or from one thing to another. [R.] "An unadvised
transiliency." Glanvill.
