TRES-TYNE
Tres"-tyne`, n. Etym: [L. tris, tres, three + E. tyne.] (Zo√∂l.)

Defn: In the antler of a stag, the third tyne above the base. This
tyne appears in the third year. In those deer in which the brow tyne
does not divide, the tres-tyne is the second tyne above the base. See
Illust. under Rucervine, and under Rusine.
