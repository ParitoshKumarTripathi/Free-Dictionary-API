TETRARCH
Te"trarch, n. Etym: [L. tetrarches, Gr. Tetra-) + tétrarque. See
Arch, a.] (Rom. Antiq.)

Defn: A Roman governor of the fourth part of a province; hence, any
subordinate or dependent prince; also, a petty king or sovereign.
TETRARCH

=====
Te"trarch, a.

Defn: Four. [Obs.] Fuller.
