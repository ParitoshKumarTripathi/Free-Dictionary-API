PALEOTHERIUM
Pa`le*o*the"ri*um, n. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. (Paleon.)

Defn: An extinct genus of herbivorous Tertiary mammals, once supposed
to have resembled the tapir in form, but now known to have had a more
slender form, with a long neck like that of a llama. [Written also
Palæotherium.]
