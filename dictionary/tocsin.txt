TOCSIN
Toc"sin, n. Etym: [F., fr. OF. toquier to touch, F. toquer
(originally, a dialectic form of F. toucher) + seint (for sein) a
bell, LL. signum, fr. L. signum a sign, signal. See Touch, and Sign.]

Defn: An alarm bell, or the ringing of a bell for the purpose of
alarm.
The loud tocsin tolled their last alarm. Campbell.
