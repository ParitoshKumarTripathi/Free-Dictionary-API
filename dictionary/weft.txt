WEFT
Weft, obs.

Defn: imp. & p. p. of Wave.
WEFT

=====
Weft, n. Etym: [Cf. Waif.]

Defn: A thing waved, waived, or cast away; a waif. [Obs.] "A forlorn
weft." Spenser.
WEFT

=====
Weft, n. Etym: [AS. weft, wefta, fr. wefan, to weave. See Weave.]

1. The woof of cloth; the threads that cross the warp from selvage to
selvage; the thread carried by the shuttle in weaving.

2. A web; a thing woven.
