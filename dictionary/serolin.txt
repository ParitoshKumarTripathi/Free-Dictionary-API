SEROLIN
Ser"o*lin, n. Etym: [Serum + L. oleum oil.] (Physiol. Chem.)
(a) A peculiar fatty substance found in the blood, probably a mixture
of fats, cholesterin, etc.
(b) A body found in fecal matter and thought to be formed in the
intestines from the cholesterin of the bile; -- called also
stercorin, and stercolin.
