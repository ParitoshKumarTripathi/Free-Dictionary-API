SPECULATORY
Spec"u*la*to*ry, a. Etym: [L. speculatorius belonging to spies or
scouts.]

1. Intended or adapted for viewing or espying; having oversight. T.
Warton.

2. Exercising speculation; speculative. T. Carew.
