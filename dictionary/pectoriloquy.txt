PECTORILOQUY
Pec`to*ril"o*quy, n. Etym: [L. pectus, -oris, the breast + loqui to
speak: cf. F. pectoriloquie.] (Med.)

Defn: The distinct articulation of the sounds of a patient's voice,
heard on applying the ear to the chest in auscultation. It usually
indicates some morbid change in the lungs or pleural cavity.
