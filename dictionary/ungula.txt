UNGULA
Un"gu*la, n.; pl. Ungul√¶. Etym: [L., a claw, hoof, from unguis a
nail, claw, hoof.]

1. A hoof, claw, or talon.

2. (Geom.)

Defn: A section or part of a cylinder, cone, or other solid of
revolution, cut off by a plane oblique to the base; -- so called from
its resemblance to the hoof of a horse.

3. (Bot.)

Defn: Same as Unguis, 3. Spherical ungula (Geom.), a part of a sphere
bounded by two planes intersecting in a diameter and by a line of the
surface of the sphere.
