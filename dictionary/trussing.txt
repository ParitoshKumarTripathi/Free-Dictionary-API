TRUSSING
Truss"ing, n.

1. (Arch. & Engin.)

Defn: The timbers, etc., which form a truss, taken collectively.
Weale.

2. (Arch. & Engin.)

Defn: The art of stiffening or bracing a set of timbers, or the like,
by putting in struts, ties, etc., till it has something of the
character of a truss.

3. The act of a hawk, or other bird of prey, in seizing its quarry,
and soaring with it into air. [Obs.]
