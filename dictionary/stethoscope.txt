STETHOSCOPE
Steth"o*scope, n. Etym: [Gr. -scope: cf. F. stéthoscope.] (Med.)

Defn: An instrument used in auscultation for examining the organs of
the chest, as the heart and lungs, by conveying to the ear of the
examiner the sounds produced in the thorax.
STETHOSCOPE

=====
Steth"o*scope, v. t.

Defn: To auscultate, or examine, with a stethoscope. M. W. Savage.
