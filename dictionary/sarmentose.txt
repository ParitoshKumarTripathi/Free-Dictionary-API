SARMENTOSE
Sar`men*tose" ( or ), a. Etym: [L. sarmentosus: cf. F. sarmenteux.
See Sarment.] (Bot.)
(a) Long and filiform, and almost naked, or having only leaves at the
joints where it strikes root; as, a sarmentose stem.
(b) Bearing sarments; sarmentaceous.
