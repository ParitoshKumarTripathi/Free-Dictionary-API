SCUTIPED
Scu"ti*ped, a. Etym: [L. scutum a shield + pes, pedis, a foot: cf. F.
scutipède.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: Having the anterior surface of the tarsus covered with
scutella, or transverse scales, in the form of incomplete bands
terminating at a groove on each side; -- said of certain birds.
