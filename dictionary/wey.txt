WEY
Wey, n.

Defn: Way; road; path. [Obs.] Chaucer.
WEY

=====
Wey, v. t. & i.

Defn: To weigh. [Obs.] Chaucer.
WEY

=====
Wey, n. Etym: [OE. weye, AS. w weight. Weight.]

Defn: A certain measure of weight. [Eng.] "A weye of Essex cheese."
Piers Plowman.

Note: A wey is 6 Simmonds.
