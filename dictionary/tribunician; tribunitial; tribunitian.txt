TRIBUNICIAN; TRIBUNITIAL; TRIBUNITIAN
Trib`u*ni"cian, Trib`u*ni"tial, Trib`u*ni*tian, a. Etym: [L.
tribunicius, tribunitius: cf. F. tribunitien.]

Defn: Of or pertaining to tribunes; befitting a tribune; as,
tribunitial power or authority. Dryden.
A kind of tribunician veto, forbidding that which is recognized to be
wrong. Hare.
