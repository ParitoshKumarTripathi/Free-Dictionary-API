USUFRUCTUARY
U`su*fruc"tu*a*ry, n. Etym: [L. usufructuarius.] (Law)

Defn: A person who has the use of property and reaps the profits of
it. Wharton.
USUFRUCTUARY

=====
U`su*fruc"tu*a*ry, a. (Law)

Defn: Of or pertaining to a usufruct; having the nature of a
usufruct.
The ordinary graces bequeathed by Christ to his church, as the
usufructuary property of all its members. Coleridge.
