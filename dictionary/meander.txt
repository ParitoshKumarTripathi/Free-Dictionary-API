MEANDER

3. (Arch.)

MEANDER

=====
Me*an"der, v. t.

Defn: To wind, turn, or twist; to make flexuous. Dryton.
MEANDER

=====
Me*an"der, v. i. [imp. & p. p. Meandered; p. pr. & vb. n.
Meandering.]

Defn: To wind or turn in a course or passage; to be intricate.
Five miles meandering with a mazy motion Through wood and dale the
sacred river ran. Coleridge.
