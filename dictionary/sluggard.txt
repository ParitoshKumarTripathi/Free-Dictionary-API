SLUGGARD
Slug"gard, n. Etym: [Slug + -ard.]

Defn: A person habitually lazy, idle, and inactive; a drone.
Go to the ant, thou sluggard; considered her ways, and be wise. Prov.
vi. 6.
SLUGGARD

=====
Slug"gard, a.

Defn: Sluggish; lazy. Dryden.
