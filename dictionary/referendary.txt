REFERENDARY
Ref`er*en"da*ry (rf`r*n"d*r), n. Etym: [LL. referendarius, fr. L.
referendus to be referred, gerundive of referre: cf. F. référendaire.
See Refer.]

1. One to whose decision a cause is referred; a referee. [Obs.]
Bacon.

2. An officer who delivered the royal answer to petitions.
"Referendaries, or masters of request." Harmar.

3. Formerly, an officer of state charged with the duty of procuring
and dispatching diplomas and decrees.
