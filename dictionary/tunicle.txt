TUNICLE
Tu"ni*cle, n. Etym: [L. tunicula a little tunic, coat, or membrane,
dim. of tunica a tunic: cf. OF. tunicle.]

1. A slight natural covering; an integument.
The tunicles that make the ball or apple of the eye. Holland.

2. (R. C. Ch.)

Defn: A short, close-fitting vestment worn by bishops under the
dalmatic, and by subdeacons.
