PHALANGOIDEA
Phal`an*goi"de*a, n. pl. Etym: [NL., from Phalangium the daddy
longlegs (see Phalangious) + Gr. (Zoöl.)

Defn: A division of Arachnoidea, including the daddy longlegs or
harvestman (Phalangium) and many similar kinds. They have long,
slender, many-jointed legs; usually a rounded, segmented abdomen; and
chelate jaws. They breathe by tracheæ. Called also Phalangides,
Phalangidea, Phalangiida, and Opilionea.
