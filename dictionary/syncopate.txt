SYNCOPATE
Syn"co*pate, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Syncopated; p. pr. & vb. n.
Syncopating.] Etym: [LL. syncopatus, p.p. of syncopare to syncopate,
to swoon. See Syncope.]

1. (Gram.)

Defn: To contract, as a word, by taking one or more letters or
syllables from the middle; as, "Gloster" is a syncopated form of
"Gloucester."

2. (Mus.)

Defn: To commence, as a tone, on an unaccented part of a measure, and
continue it into the following accented part, so that the accent is
driven back upon the weak part and the rhythm drags.
