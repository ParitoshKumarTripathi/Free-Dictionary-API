RONCADOR
Ron`ca*dor", n. Etym: [Sp., a snorer, fr. roncar to snore. So called
in allusion to the grunting noise made by them on being taken from
the water. ] (Zoöl.)

Defn: Any one of several species of California sciænoid food fishes,
especially Roncador Stearnsi, which is an excellent market fish, and
the red roncador (Corvina, or Johnius, saturna).
