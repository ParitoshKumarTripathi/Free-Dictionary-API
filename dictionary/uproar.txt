UPROAR
Up"roar, n. Etym: [D. oproer; akin to G. aufruhr, Dan. oprör, Sw.
uppror; D. op up + roeren to stir; akin to AS. hr to stir, hr
stirring, active, G. rühren to stir, OHG. ruoren, Icel. hræra, Dan.
röre, Sw. röra. Cf. Rearmouse.]

Note: [In verse, sometimes accented on the second syllable.]

Defn: Great tumult; violent disturbance and noise; noisy confusion;
bustle and clamor.
But the Jews which believed not, . . . set all the city on an uproar.
Acts xvii. 5.
UPROAR

=====
Up*roar", v. t.

Defn: To throw into uproar or confusion. [Obs.] "Uproar the universal
peace." Shak.
UPROAR

=====
Up*roar", v. i.

Defn: To make an uproar. [R.] Carlyle.
