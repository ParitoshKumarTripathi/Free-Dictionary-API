HISTRIONIC; HISTRIONICAL
His`tri*on"ic, His`tri*on"ic*al, a. Etym: [L. histrionicus: cf. F.
histronique. See Histrion.]

Defn: Of or relating to the stage or a stageplayer; befitting a
theatre; theatrical; -- sometimes in a bad sense.
 -- His`tri*on"ic*al*ly, adv.
Tainted with false and histrionic feeling. De Quincey.
