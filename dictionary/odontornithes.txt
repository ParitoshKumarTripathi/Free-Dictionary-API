ODONTORNITHES
O*don`tor*ni*"thes, n. pl. Etym: [NL., fr.Gr. 'odoy`s, 'odo`ntos, a
tooth + (Paleon.)

Defn: A group of Mesozoic birds having the jaws armed with teeth, as
in most other vertebrates. They have been divided into three orders:
Odontolcæ, Odontotormæ, and Saururæ.
