HYPOGENE
Hyp"o*gene, a. Etym: [Pref. hypo- + the root of Gr. hypog√®ne.]
(Geol.)

Defn: Formed or crystallized at depths the earth's surface; -- said
of granite, gneiss, and other rocks, whose crystallization is
believed of have taken place beneath a great thickness of overlying
rocks. Opposed to epigene.
