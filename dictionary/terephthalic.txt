TEREPHTHALIC
Ter`eph*thal"ic, a. Etym: [Terebene + phthalic.] (Chem.)

Defn: Of, pertaining to, or designating, a dibasic acid of the
aromatic series, metameric with phthalic acid, and obtained, as a
tasteless white crystalline powder, by the oxidation of oil of
turpentine; -- called also paraphthalic acid. Cf. Phthalic.
