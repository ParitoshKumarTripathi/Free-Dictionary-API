PROCESSIONARY
Pro*ces"sion*a*ry, a. Etym: [Cf. LL. processionarius, F.
processionnaire.]

Defn: Pertaining to a procession; consisting in processions; as,
processionary service. Processionary moth (Zoöl.), any moth of the
genus Cnethocampa, especially C. processionea of Europe, whose larvæ
make large webs on oak trees, and go out to feed in regular order.
They are covered with stinging hairs.
