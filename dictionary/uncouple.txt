UNCOUPLE
Un*cou"ple, v. t. Etym: [1st pref. un- + couple.]

Defn: To loose, as dogs, from their couples; also, to set loose; to
disconnect; to disjoin; as, to uncouple railroad cars.
UNCOUPLE

=====
Un*cou"ple, v. i.

Defn: To roam at liberty. [Obs.] Chaucer.
