UNTO
Un"to, prep. Etym: [OE. unto; un- (only in unto, until) unto, as far
as + to to; this un- is akin to AS. und OS. und until, conj. (cf. OS.
unt unto, OHG. unzi), Goth. und unto, until. See To, and cf. Until.]

1. To; -- now used only in antiquated, formal, or scriptural style.
See To.

2. Until; till. [Obs.] "He shall abide it unto the death of the
priest." Num. xxxv. 25.
UNTO

=====
Un"to, conj.

Defn: Until; till. [Obs.] "Unto this year be gone." Chaucer.
