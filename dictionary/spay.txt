SPAY
Spay, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Spayed; p. pr. & vb. n. Spaying.] Etym:
[Cf. Armor. spac'hein, spaza to geld, W. dyspaddu to geld, L. spado a
eunuch, Gr.

Defn: To remove or extirpate the ovaries of, as a sow or a bitch; to
castrate (a female animal).
