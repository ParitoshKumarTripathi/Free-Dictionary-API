PLOP
Plop, v. i.  [imp. & p. p. Plopped; p. pr. & vb. n. Plopping.]
[Imitative.]

Defn: To fall, drop, or move in any way, with a sudden splash or
slap, as on the surface of water.

The body plopped up, turning on its side.
Kipling.
PLOP

=====
Plop, n.

Defn: Act of plopping; the sound made in plopping.
