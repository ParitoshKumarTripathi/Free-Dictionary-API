RESIDUUM
Re*sid"u*um (-m), n. Etym: [L. See Residue.]

Defn: That which is left after any process of separation or
purification; that which remains after certain specified deductions
are made; residue.
"I think so," is the whole residuum . . . after evaporating the
prodigious pretensions of the zealot demagogue. L. Taylor.
