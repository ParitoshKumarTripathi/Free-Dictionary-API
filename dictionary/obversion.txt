OBVERSION
Ob*ver"sion, n. Etym: [L. obversio a turning towards.]

1. The act of turning toward or downward.

2. (Logic)

Defn: The act of immediate inference, by which we deny the opposite
of anything which has been affirmed; as, all men are mortal; then, by
obversion, no men are immortal. This is also described as "immediate
inference by privative conception." Bain.
