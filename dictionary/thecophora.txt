THECOPHORA
The*coph"o*ra, n. pl. Etym: [NL., from Gr. (Zoöl.)

Defn: A division of hydroids comprising those which have the
hydranths in thecæ and the gonophores in capsules. The campanularians
and sertularians are examples. Called also Thecata. See Illust. under
Hydroidea.
