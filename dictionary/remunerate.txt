REMUNERATE
Re*mu"ner*ate (-t), v. t. [imp. & p. p. Remunerated (-"td); p. pr. &
vb. n. Remunerating.] Etym: [L. remuneratus, p. p. of remunerare,
remunerari; pref. re- re- + munerare, munerari, to give, present,
from munus, muneris, a gift, present. Cf. Munificent.]

Defn: To pay an equivalent to for any service, loss, expense, or
other sacrifice; to recompense; to requite; as, to remunerate men for
labor.

Syn.
 -- To reward; recompense; compensate; satisfy; requite; repay; pay;
reimburse.
