HOMOTYPE
Hom"o*type, n. Etym: [Homo- + -type.] (Biol.)

Defn: That which has the same fundamental type of structure with
something else; thus, the right arm is the homotype of the right leg;
one arm is the homotype of the other, etc. Owen.
