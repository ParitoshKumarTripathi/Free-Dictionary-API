WASHERWOMAN
Wash"er*wom`an, n.; pl. Washerwomen (.

1. A woman who washes clothes, especially for hire, or for others.

2. (Zoöl.)

Defn: The pied wagtail; -- so called in allusion to its beating the
water with its tail while tripping along the leaves of water plants.
[Prov. Eng.]
