TYROTOXICON
Tyr`o*tox"i*con, n. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. Toxic.] (Physiol. Chem.)

Defn: A ptomaine discovered by Vaughan in putrid cheese and other
dairy products, and producing symptoms similar to cholera infantum.
Chemically, it appears to be related to, or identical with,
diazobenzol.
