VERSICLE
Ver"si*cle, n. Etym: [L. versiculus, dim. of versus. See Verse.]

Defn: A little verse; especially, a short verse or text said or sung
in public worship by the priest or minister, and followed by a
response from the people.
The psalms were in number fifteen, . . . being digested into
versicles. Strype.
