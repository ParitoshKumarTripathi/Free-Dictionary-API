POONAH PAINTING
Poo"nah paint`ing. [From Poona, in Bombay Province, India.]

Defn: A style of painting, popular in England in the 19th century, in
which a thick opaque color is applied without background and with
scarcely any shading, to thin paper, producing flowers, birds, etc.,
in imitation of Oriental work. Hence: Poonah brush, paper, painter,
etc.
