SPONDEE
Spon"dee, n. Etym: [L. spondeus, Gr. spondée. So called because at
libations slow, solemn melodies were used, chiefly in this meter.]
(pros.)

Defn: A poetic foot of two long syllables, as in the Latin word
leges.
