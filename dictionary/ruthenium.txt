RUTHENIUM
Ru*the"ni*um, n. Etym: [NL. So named from the Ruthenians, a Little
Russian people, as coming from Russia, the metal having been found in
the Ural mountains.] (Chem.)

Defn: A rare element of the light platinum group, found associated
with platinum ores, and isolated as a hard, brittle steel-gray metal
which is very infusible. Symbol Ru. Atomic weight 103.5. Specific
gravity 12.26. See Platinum metals, under Platinum.
