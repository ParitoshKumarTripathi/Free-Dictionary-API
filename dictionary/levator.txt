LEVATOR
Le*va"tor, n. Etym: [NL., fr. L. levare to raise. See Lever, n.]

1. (Anat.)

Defn: A muscle that serves to raise some part, as the lip or the
eyelid.

2. (Surg.)

Defn: A surgical instrument used to raise a depressed part of the
skull.
