PHOTOTROPISM
Pho*tot"ro*pism, n.  [Photo-+ Gr.  to turn.] (Plant Physiol.)

Defn: The tendency of growing plant organs to move or curve under the
influence of light. In ordinary use the term is practically
synonymous with heliotropism.
