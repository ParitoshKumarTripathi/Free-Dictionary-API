WILY
Wil"y, a. [Compar. Wilier; superl. Wiliest.] Etym: [From Wile.]

Defn: Full of wiles, tricks, or stratagems; using craft or stratagem
to accomplish a purpose; mischievously artful; subtle. "Wily and
wise." Chaucer. "The wily snake." Milton.
This false, wily, doubling disposition of mind. South.

Syn.
 -- Cunning; artful; sly; crafty. See Cunning.
