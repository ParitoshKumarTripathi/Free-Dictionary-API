SAUCISSON; SAUCISSE
Sau`cis`son", Sau`cisse", n. Etym: [F., fr. saucisse sausage. See
Sausage.]

1. (Mining or Gun.)

Defn: A long and slender pipe or bag, made of cloth well pitched, or
of leather, filled with powder, and used to communicate fire to
mines, caissons, bomb chests, etc.

2. (Fort.)

Defn: A fascine of more than ordinary length.
