INTERCROP
In"ter*crop`, v. t. & i. [imp. & p. p. -cropped; p. pr. & vb. n. -
cropping.] (Agric.)

Defn: To cultivate by planting simultaneous crops in alternate rows;
as, to intercrop an orchard. Also, to use for catch crops at seasons
when the ground is not covered by crops of the regular rotation.
INTERCROP

=====
In"ter*crop`, n. (Agric.)

Defn: A crop grown among or between  the rows of another crop; a
catch crop.
