TERATOLOGY
Ter`a*tol"o*gy, n. Etym: [Gr. -logy: cf. Gr. tératologie.]

1. That branch of biological science which treats of monstrosities,
malformations, or deviations from the normal type of structure,
either in plants or animals.

2. Affectation of sublimity; bombast. [Obs.] Bailey.
