PARALLELOPIPED
Par`al*lel`o*pi"ped, n. Etym: [Gr. parallélopipède.] (Geom.)

Defn: A solid, the faces of which are six parallelograms, the
opposite pairs being parallel, and equal to each other; a prism whose
base is a parallelogram.
