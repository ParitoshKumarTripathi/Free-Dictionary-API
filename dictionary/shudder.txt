SHUDDER
Shud"der, v. i. [imp. & p. p. Shuddered;p. pr. & vb. n. Shuddering.]
Etym: [OE. shoderen, schuderen; akin to LG. schuddern, D. schudden to
shake, OS. skuddian, G. schaudern to shudder, sch端tteln to shake,
sch端tten to pour, to shed, OHG. scutten, scuten, to shake.]

Defn: To tremble or shake with fear, horrer, or aversion; to shiver
with cold; to quake. "With shuddering horror pale." Milton.
The shuddering tennant of the frigid zone. Goldsmith.
SHUDDER

=====
Shud"der, n.

Defn: The act of shuddering, as with fear. Shak.
