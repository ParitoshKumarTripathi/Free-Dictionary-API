HISTOHAEMATIN
His`to*hæm"a*tin, n. Etym: [Gr. "isto`s tissue + E. hæmatin.]
(Physiol.)

Defn: One of a class of respiratory pigments, widely distributed in
the animal kingdom, capable of ready oxidation and reduction.
