SPRIT
Sprit, v. t. Etym: [Akin to G. spritzen, spr체tzen. See Sprit, v. i.]

Defn: To throw out with force from a narrow orifice; to eject; to
spurt out. [Obs.] Sir T. Browne.
SPRIT

=====
Sprit, v. i. Etym: [AS. spryttan to sprout, but. See Sprout, v. i.,
and cf. Spurt, v. t., Sprit a spar.]

Defn: To sprout; to bud; to germinate, as barley steeped for malt.
SPRIT

=====
Sprit, n.

Defn: A shoot; a sprout. [Obs.] Mortimer.
SPRIT

=====
Sprit, n. Etym: [OE. spret, AS. spre처t a sprit; spear; akin to D.
spriet, and E. sprout, sprit, v.t. & i. See Sprout, v. i.] (Naut.)

Defn: A small boom, pole, or spar, which crosses the sail of a boat
diagonally from the mast to the upper aftmost corner, which it is
used to extend and elevate.
