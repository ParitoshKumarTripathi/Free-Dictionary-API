SHEAL
Sheal, n.

Defn: Same as Sheeling. [Scot.]
SHEAL

=====
Sheal, v. t.

Defn: To put under a sheal or shelter. [Scot.]
SHEAL

=====
Sheal, v. t. Etym: [See Shell.]

Defn: To take the husks or pods off from; to shell; to empty of its
contents, as a husk or a pod. [Obs. or Prov.Eng. & Scot.] Jamieson.
That's a shealed peascod. Shak.
SHEAL

=====
Sheal, n.

Defn: A shell or pod. [Obs. or Prov.Eng.]
