SIGNIFICAVIT
Sig`ni*fi*ca"vit, n. Etym: [L., (he) has signified, perf. ind. of
significare to signify.] (Eng. Eccl. Law)

Defn: Formerly, a writ issuing out of chancery, upon certificate
given by the ordinary, of a man's standing excommunicate by the space
of forty days, for the laying him up in prison till he submit himself
to the authority of the church. Crabb.
