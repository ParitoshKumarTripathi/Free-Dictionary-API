TONGS
Tongs, n. pl. Etym: [OE. tonge, tange, AS. tange; akin to D. tang, G.
zanga, OHG. zanga, Don. tang, Sw. tng, Icel. tng, Gr. dadaTang a
strong taste, anything projecting.]

Defn: An instrument, usually of metal, consisting of two parts, or
long shafts, jointed together at or near one end, or united by an
elastic bow, used for handling things, especially hot coals or
metals; -- often called a pair of tongs.
