QUARTER-SAW
Quar"ter-saw`, v. t.  [imp. & p. p. Quarter-sawed  -sawn; p. pr. &
vb. n. Quarter-sawing.]

Defn: To saw (a log) into quarters; specif., to saw into quarters and
then into boards, as by cutting alternately from each face of a
quarter, to secure lumber that will warp relatively little or show
the grain advantageously.
