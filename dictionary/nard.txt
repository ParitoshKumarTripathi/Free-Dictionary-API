NARD
Nard, n. Etym: [AS., fr. L. nardus, Gr. nÃªrd, Per. nard, Scr.
nalada.]

1. (Bot.)

Defn: An East Indian plant (Nardostachys Jatamansi) of the Valerian
family, used from remote ages in Oriental perfumery.

2. An ointment prepared partly from this plant. See Spikenard.

3. (Bot.)

Defn: A kind of grass (Nardus stricta) of little value, found in
Europe and Asia.
