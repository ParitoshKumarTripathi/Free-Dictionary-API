POLYPHONY
Po*lyph"o*ny, n. Etym: [Gr.

1. Multiplicity of sounds, as in the reverberations of an echo.

2. Plurality of sounds and articulations expressed by the same vocal
sign.

3. (Mus.)

Defn: Composition in mutually related, equally important parts which
share the melody among them; contrapuntal composition; -- opposed to
homophony, in which the melody is given to one part only, the others
filling out the harmony. See Counterpoint.
