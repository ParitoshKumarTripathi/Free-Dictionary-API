OVERHAND
O"ver*hand`, n.

Defn: The upper hand; advantage; superiority; mastery.
He had gotten thereby a great overhand on me. Sir T. More.
OVERHAND

=====
O"ver*hand`, a.

1. (Sewing)

Defn: Over and over; -- applied to a style of sewing, or to a seam,
in which two edges, usually selvedges, are sewed together by passing
each stitch over both.

2. (Baseball, Cricket, etc.)

Defn: Done (as pitching or bowling) with the hand higher than the
elbow, or the arm above, or higher than, the shoulder. Overhand knot.
See Illustration of Knot.
OVERHAND

=====
O"ver*hand`, adv.

Defn: In an overhand manner or style.
