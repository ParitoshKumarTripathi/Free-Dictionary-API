TETRATOMIC
Tet`ra*tom"ic, a. Etym: [Tetra- + atomic.] (Chem.)
(a) Consisting of four atoms; having four atoms in the molecule, as
phosphorus and arsenic.
(b) Having a valence of four; quadrivalent; tetravalent; sometimes,
in a specific sense, having four hydroxyl groups, whether acid or
basic.
