QUIRBOILLY
Quir"boil*ly`, n. Etym: [OE. cuir bouilli.]

Defn: Leather softened by boiling so as to take any required shape.
Upon drying, it becomes exceedingly hard, and hence was formerly used
for armor. [Obs.] "His jambeux were of quyrboilly." Chaucer.
