QUO WARRANTO
Quo" war*ran"to. Etym: [So called from the Law L. words quo warranto
(by what authority), in the original Latin form of the writ. See
Which, and Warrant.] (Law)

Defn: A writ brought before a proper tribunal, to inquire by what
warrant a person or a corporation acts, or exercises certain powers.
Blackstone.

Note: An information in the nature of a quo warranto is now common as
a substitute for the writ. Wharton.
