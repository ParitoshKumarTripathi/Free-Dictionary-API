UNNERVE
Un*nerve", v. t. Etym: [1st pref. un- + nerve.]

Defn: To deprive of nerve, force, or strength; to weaken; to
enfeeble; as, to unnerve the arm.
Unequal match'd, . . . The unnerved father falls. Shak.
