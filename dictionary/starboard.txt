STARBOARD
Star"board`, n. Etym: [OE. sterbord, AS. ste√≥rbord, i.e., steer
board. See Steer, v. t., Board of a vessel, and cf. Larboard.]
(Naut.)

Defn: That side of a vessel which is one of the right hand of a
person who stands on board facing the bow; -- opposed to Ant:
larboard, or Ant: port.
STARBOARD

=====
Star"board`, a. (Naut.)

Defn: Pertaining to the right-hand side of a ship; being or lying on
the right side; as, the starboard quarter; starboard tack.
STARBOARD

=====
Star"board`, v. t. (Naut.)

Defn: To put to the right, or starboard, side of a vessel; as, to
starboard the helm.
