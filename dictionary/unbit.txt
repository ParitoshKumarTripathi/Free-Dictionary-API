UNBIT
Un*bit", v. t. [imp. & p. p. Unbitted; p. pr. & vb. n. Unbitting.]
Etym: [1st pref. un- + bit.] (Naut.)

Defn: To remove the turns of (a rope or cable) from the bits; as, to
unbit a cable. Totten.
