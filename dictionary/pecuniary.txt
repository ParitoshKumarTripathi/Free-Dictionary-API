PECUNIARY
Pe*cun"ia*ry, a. Etym: [L. pecuniarius, fr. pecunia money, orig.,
property in cattle, fr. pecus cattle: cf. F. pécuniaire. See Fee, and
cf. Peculiar.]

1. Relating to money; monetary; as, a pecuniary penalty; a pecuniary
reward. Burke.
