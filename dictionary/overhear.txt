OVERHEAR
O`ver*hear", v. t. [imp. & p. p. Overheard; p. pr. & vb. n.
Overhearing.] Etym: [AS. oferhiéran.]

1. To hear more of (anything) than was intended to be heard; to hear
by accident or artifice. Shak.

2. To hear again. ShaK.
