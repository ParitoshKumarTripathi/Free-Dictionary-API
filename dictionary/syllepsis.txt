SYLLEPSIS
Syl*lep"sis, n. Etym: [L., fr. Gr. sy`llhpsis a taking together, from
syllable, n.]

1. (Rhet.)

Defn: A figure of speech by which a word is used in a literal and
metaphorical sense at the same time.

2. (Gram.)

Defn: The agreement of a verb or adjective with one, rather than
another, of two nouns, with either of which it might agree in gender,
number, etc.; as, rex et regina beati.
