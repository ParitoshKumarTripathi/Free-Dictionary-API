HILUM
Hi"lum, n. Etym: [L., a little thing, trifle.]

1. (Bot.)

Defn: The eye of a bean or other seed; the mark or scar at the point
of attachment of an ovule or seed to its base or support; -- called
also hile.

2. (Anat.)

Defn: The part of a gland, or similar organ, where the blood vessels
and nerves enter; the hilus; as, the hilum of the kidney.
