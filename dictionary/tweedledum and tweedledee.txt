TWEEDLEDUM AND TWEEDLEDEE
Twee"dle*dum` and Twee"dle*dee`.

Defn: Two things practically alike; -- a phrase coined by John Byrom
(1692-1793) in his satire "On the Feuds between Handel and
Bononcini."
