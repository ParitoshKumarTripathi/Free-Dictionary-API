POTT'S DISEASE
Pott's" dis*ease". (Med.)

Defn: Caries of the vertebræ, frequently resulting in curvature of
the spine and paralysis of the lower extremities; -- so named from
Percival Pott, an English surgeon. Pott's fracture, a fracture of the
lower end of the fibula, with displacement of the tibia. Dunglison.
