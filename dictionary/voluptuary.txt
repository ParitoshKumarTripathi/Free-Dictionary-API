VOLUPTUARY
Vo*lup"tu*a*ry, n.; pl. Voluptuaries. Etym: [L. voluptuarius or
voluptarius, fr. voluptas pleasure.]

Defn: A voluptuous person; one who makes his physical enjoyment his
chief care; one addicted to luxury, and the gratification of sensual
appetites.
A good-humored, but hard-hearted, voluptuary. Sir W. Scott.

Syn.
 -- Sensualist; epicure.
VOLUPTUARY

=====
Vo*lup"tu*a*ry, a.

Defn: Voluptuous; luxurious.
