PRESUPPOSITION
Pre*sup`po*si"tion, n. Etym: [Pref. pre- + supposition: cf. F.
présupposition.]

1. The act of presupposing; an antecedent implication; presumption.

2. That which is presupposed; a previous supposition or surmise.
