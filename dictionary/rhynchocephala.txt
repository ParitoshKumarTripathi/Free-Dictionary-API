RHYNCHOCEPHALA
Rhyn`cho*ceph"a*la, n. pl. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. (Zoöl.)

Defn: An order of reptiles having biconcave vertebræ, immovable
quadrate bones, and many other peculiar osteological characters.
Hatteria is the only living genus, but numerous fossil genera are
known, some of which are among the earliest of reptiles. See
Hatteria. Called also Rhynchocephalia.
