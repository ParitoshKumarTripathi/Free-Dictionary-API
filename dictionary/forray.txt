FORRAY
For"ray ( or ), v. t. Etym: [OE. forrayen. See Foray.]

Defn: To foray; to ravage; to pillage.
For they that morn had forrayed all the land. Fairfax.
FORRAY

=====
For"ray, n.

Defn: The act of ravaging; a ravaging; a predatory excursion. See
Foray.
