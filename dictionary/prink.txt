PRINK
Prink, v. i. [imp. & p. p. Prinked; p. pr. & vb. n. Prinking.] Etym:
[Probably a nasalized form of prick. See Prick, v. t., and cf. Prig,
Prank.]

Defn: To dress or adjust one's self for show; to prank.
PRINK

=====
Prink, v. t.

Defn: To prank or dress up; to deck fantastically. "And prink their
hair with daisies." Cowper.
