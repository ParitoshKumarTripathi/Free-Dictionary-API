QUEAN
Quean, n. Etym: [Originally, a woman, AS. cwene; akin to OS. quena,
OHG. quena, Icel. kona, Goth qin, and AS. cwén, also to Gr. gna
goddess. Cf. Queen.]

1. A woman; a young or unmarried woman; a girl. [Obs. or Scot.]
Chaucer.

2. A low woman; a wench; a slut. "The dread of every scolding quean."
Gay.
