REALTY
Re"al*ty, n. Etym: [OF. réalté, LL. regalitas, fr. L. regalis. See
Regal.]

1. Royalty. [Obs.] Chaucer.

2. Loyalty; faithfulness. [R.] Milton.
REALTY

=====
Re"al*ty, n. Etym: [Contr. from 1st Reality.]

1. Realty. [Obs.] Dr. H. More.

2. (Law)
(a) Immobility, or the fixed, permanent nature of real property; as,
chattels which savor of the realty; -- so written in legal language
for reality.
(b) Real estate; a piece of real property. Blackstone.
