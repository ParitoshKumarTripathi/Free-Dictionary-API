SCROYLE
Scroyle, n. Etym: [Cf. OF. escrouselle a kind of vermin, escrouelles,
pl., scrofula, F. écrouelles, fr. (assumed) LL. scrofulae. See
Scrofula, and cf. Cruels.]

Defn: A mean fellow; a wretch. [Obs.] hak.
