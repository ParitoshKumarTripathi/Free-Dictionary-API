REZ-DE-CHAUSSEE
Rez`-de-chaus`sée", n. [F., lit., level of the street. See Raze, v.
t., and Causey.] (Arch.)

Defn: The ground story of a building, either on a level with the
street or raised slightly above it; -- said esp. of buildings on the
continent of Europe.

Tier above tier of neat apartments rise over the little shops which
form the rez-de-chaussée.
The Century.
