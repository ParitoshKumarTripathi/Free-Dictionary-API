TRIENNIAL
Tri*en"ni*al, a. Etym: [L. triennium the space of three years; tri-
(see Tri-) + annus year. See Annual.]

1. Continuing three years; as, triennial parliaments; a triennial
reign. Howell.

2. Happening, coming about, or appearing once in every three years;
as, triennial elections; a triennial catalogue; a triennial
visitation. T. Warton.
TRIENNIAL

=====
Tri*en"ni*al, n.

Defn: Something which takes place or appears once in three years.
