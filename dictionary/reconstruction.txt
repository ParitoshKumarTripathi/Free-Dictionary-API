RECONSTRUCTION
Re`con*struc"tion (-strk"shn), n.

1. The act of constructing again; the state of being reconstructed.

2. (U.S. Politics)

Defn: The act or process of reorganizing the governments of the
States which had passed ordinances of secession, and of
reëstablishing their constitutional relations to the national
government, after the close of the Civil War.
