OBLONG
Ob"long, a. Etym: [L. oblongus; ob (see Ob-) + longus long: cf. F.
oblong.]

Defn: Having greater length than breadth, esp. when rectangular.
OBLONG

=====
Ob"long, n.

Defn: A rectangular figure longer than it is broad; hence, any figure
longer than it is broad.
The best figure of a garden I esteem an oblong upon a descent. Sir W.
Temple.
