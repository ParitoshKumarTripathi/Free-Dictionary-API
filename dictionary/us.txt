US
Us, pron. Etym: [OE. us, AS. ; akin to OFries. & OS. , D. ons, G.
uns, Icel. & Sw. oss, Dan. os, Goth. uns, L. nos we, us, Gr. nas us.
Nostrum, Our.]

Defn: The persons speaking, regarded as an object; ourselves; -- the
objective case of we. See We. "Tell us a tale." Chaucer.
Give us this day our daily bread. Matt. vi. 11.
