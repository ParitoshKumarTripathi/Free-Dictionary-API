GLUTAEUS
Glu*tæ"us, n. Etym: [NL. See Gluteal.] (Anat.)

Defn: The great muscle of the buttock in man and most mammals, and
the corresponding muscle in many lower animals.

Note: In man, the glutæus is composed of three distinct parts, which
extend and abduct the thigh, and help support the body in standing.
