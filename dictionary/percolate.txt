PERCOLATE
Per"co*late, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Percolated; p. pr. & vb. n.
Percolating.] Etym: [L. percolatus, p.p. of percolare to percolate;
per through + colare to strain.]

Defn: To cause to pass through fine interstices, as a liquor; to
filter; to strain. Sir M. Hale.
PERCOLATE

=====
Per"co*late, v. i.

Defn: To pass through fine interstices; to filter; as, water
percolates through porous stone.
