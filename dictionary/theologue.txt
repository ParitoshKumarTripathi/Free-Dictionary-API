THEOLOGUE
The"o*logue, n. Etym: [Cf. L. theologus, Gr. philologue.]

1. A theologian. Dryden.
Ye gentle theologues of calmer kind. Young.
He [Jerome] was the theologue -- and the word is designation enough.
I. Taylor.

2. A student in a theological seminary. [Written also theolog.]
[Colloq. U.S.]
