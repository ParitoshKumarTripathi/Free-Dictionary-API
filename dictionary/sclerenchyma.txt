SCLERENCHYMA
Scle*ren"chy*ma, n. Etym: [NL., from Gr. -enchyma as in parenchyma.]

1. (Bot.)

Defn: Vegetable tissue composed of short cells with thickened or
hardened walls, as in nutshells and the gritty parts of a pear. See
Sclerotic.

Note: By recent german writers and their English translation, this
term is used for liber cells. Goodale.

Defn: The hard calcareous deposit in the tissues of Anthozoa,
