RATITAE
Ra*ti"tæ, n. pl. Etym: [NL., fr. L. ratis a raft; cf. L. ratitus
marked with the figure of a raft.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: An order of birds in which the wings are small, rudimentary, or
absent, and the breastbone is destitute of a keel. The ostrich, emu,
and apteryx are examples.
