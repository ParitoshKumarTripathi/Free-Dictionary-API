TENACULUM
Te*nac"u*lum, n.; pl. L. Tenacula; E. Tenaculums. Etym: [L., a
holder, fr. tenere to hold. Cf. Tenaille.] (Surg.)

Defn: An instrument consisting of a fine, sharp hook attached to a
handle, and used mainly for taking up arteries, and the like.
