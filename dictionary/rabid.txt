RABID
Rab"id (rab"id), a. [L. rabidus, from rabere to rave. See Rage, n.]

1. Furious; raging; extremely violent.

The rabid flight
Of winds that ruin ships.
Chapman.

2. Extreme, unreasonable, or fanatical in opinion; excessively
zealous; as, a rabid socialist.

3. Affected with the distemper called rabies; mad; as, a rabid dog or
fox.

4. (Med.) Of or pertaining to rabies, or hydrophobia; as, rabid
virus.
