WESLEYANISM
Wes"ley*an*ism, n. (Eccl.)

Defn: The system of doctrines and church polity inculcated by John
Wesley (b. 1703; d. 1791), the founder of the religious sect called
Methodist; Methodism. See Methodist, n., 2.
