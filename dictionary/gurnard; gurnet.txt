GURNARD; GURNET
Gur"nard, Gur"net n. Etym: [OF. gornal, gournal, gornart, perh. akin
to F. grogner to grunt; cf. Ir. guirnead gurnard.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: One ofseveral European marine fishes, of the genus Trigla and
allied genera, having a large and spiny head, with mailed cheeks.
Some of the species are highly esteemed for food. The name is
sometimes applied to the American sea robins. [Written also gournet.]
Plyling gurnard. See under Flying.
