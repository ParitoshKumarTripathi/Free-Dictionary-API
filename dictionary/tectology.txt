TECTOLOGY
Tec*tol"o*gy, n. Etym: [Gr. -logy.] (Biol.)

Defn: A division of morphology created by Haeckel; the science of
organic individuality constituting the purely structural portion of
morphology, in which the organism is regarded as composed of organic
individuals of different orders, each organ being considered an
individual. See Promorphology, and Morphon.
