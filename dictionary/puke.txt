PUKE
Puke, v. i. [imp. & p. p. Puked; p. pr. & vb. n. Puking.] Etym: [Cf.
G. spucken to spit, and E. spew.]

Defn: To eject the contests of the stomach; to vomit; to spew.
The infant Mewling and puking in the nurse's arms. Shak.
PUKE

=====
Puke, v. t.

Defn: To eject from the stomach; to vomit up.
PUKE

=====
Puke, n.

Defn: A medicine that causes vomiting; an emetic; a vomit.
PUKE

=====
Puke, a. Etym: [Etymol. uncertain.]

Defn: Of a color supposed to be between black and russet. Shak.

Note: This color has by some been regarded as the same with puce; but
Nares questions the identity.
