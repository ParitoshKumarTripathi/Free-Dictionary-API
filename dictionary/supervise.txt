SUPERVISE
Su`per*vise", v. t. [imp. & p. p. Supervised; p. pr. & vb. n.
Supervising.] Etym: [Pref. super- + L. visere to look at attentively,
to view, surely, intens. from videre, visum, to see. Cf. Survise, and
Survey.]

1. To oversee for direction; to superintend; to inspect with
authority; as, to supervise the construction of a steam engine, or
the printing of a book.

2. To look over so as to read; to peruse. [Obs.] Shak.

Syn.
 -- See Superintend.
SUPERVISE

=====
Su`per*vise", n.

Defn: Supervision; inspection. [Obs.]
