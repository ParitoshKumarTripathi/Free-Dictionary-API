UNDERNIME
Un`der*nime", v. t. [imp. Undernom.] Etym: [OE. undernimen. See
Under, and Nim.]

1. To receive; to perceive. [Obs.]
He the savor undernom Which that the roses and the lilies cast.
Chaucer.

2. To reprove; to reprehend. [Obs.] Piers Plowman.
