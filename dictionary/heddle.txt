HEDDLE
Hed"dle, n.; pl. Heddles. Etym: [Cf. Heald.] (Weaving)

Defn: One of the sets of parallel doubled threads which, with
mounting, compose the harness employed to guide the warp threads to
the lathe or batten in a loom.
HEDDLE

=====
Hed"dle, v. t.

Defn: To draw (the warp thread) through the heddle-eyes, in weaving.
