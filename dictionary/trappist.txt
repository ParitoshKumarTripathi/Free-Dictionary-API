TRAPPIST
Trap"pist, n. Etym: [F. trappiste.] (R. C. Ch.)

Defn: A monk belonging to a branch of the Cistercian Order, which was
established by Armand de Rancé in 1660 at the monastery of La Trappe
in Normandy. Extreme austerity characterizes their discipline. They
were introduced permanently into the United States in 1848, and have
monasteries in Iowa and Kentucky.
