PYROACETIC
Pyr`o*a*ce"tic, a. Etym: [Pyro- + acetic: cf. F. pyroacétique.]
(Chem.)

Defn: Pertaining to, and designating, a substance (acetone) obtained
by the distillation of the acetates. It is now called also pyroacetic
ether, and formerly was called pyroacetic spirit.
