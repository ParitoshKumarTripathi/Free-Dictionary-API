MOPE
Mope, v. i. [imp. & p. p. Moped; p. pr. & vb. n. Moping.] Etym: [Cf.
D. moppen to pout, Prov. G. muffen to sulk.]

Defn: To be dull and spiritless. "Moping melancholy." Milton.
A sickly part of one true sense Could not so mope. Shak.
MOPE

=====
Mope, v. t.

Defn: To make spiritless and stupid. [Obs.]
MOPE

=====
Mope, n.

Defn: A dull, spiritless person. Burton.
