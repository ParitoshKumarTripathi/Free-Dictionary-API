POTENCY
Po"ten*cy, n. Etym: [L. potentia, from potens, -entis, potent. See
Potent, and cf. Potance, Potence, Puissance.]

Defn: The quality or state of being potent; physical or moral power;
inherent strength; energy; ability to effect a purpose; capability;
efficacy; influence. "Drugs of potency." Hawthorne.
A place of potency and away o' the state. Shak.
