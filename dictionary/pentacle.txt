PENTACLE
Pen"ta*cle, n. Etym: [Gr.

Defn: A figure composed of two equilateral triangles intersecting so
as to form a six-pointed star, -- used in early ornamental art, and
also with superstitious import by the astrologers and mystics of the
Middle Ages.
