HETEROLOGOUS
Het`er*ol"o*gous, a. Etym: [Hetero- + Gr.

Defn: Characterized by heterology; consisting of different elements,
or of like elements in different proportions; different; -- opposed
to homologous; as, heterologous organs. Heterologous stimulus.
(Physiol.) See under Stimulus.
 -- Heterologous tumor (Med.), a tumor differing in structure from
the normal tissues of the body.
