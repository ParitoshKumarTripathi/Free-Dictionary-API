HUSSAR
Hus*sar", n. Etym: [Hung. husz√°r, from husz twenty, because under
King Matthais I., in the fifteenth century, every twenty houses were
to furnish one horse soldier; cf. G. husar, F. houssard, hussard,

Defn: Originally, one of the national cavalry of Hungary and Croatia;
now, one of the light cavalry of European armies.
