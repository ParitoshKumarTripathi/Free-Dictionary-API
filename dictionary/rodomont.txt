RODOMONT
Rod"o*mont, n. Etym: [F. rodomont, It. rodomonte, fr. Rodomonte,
Rodamonte, a boasting hero in the "Orlando Furioso" of Ariosto, and
the "Orlando Innamorato" of Bojardo; properly, one who rolls away
mountains; Prov. It. rodare to roll away (fr. L. rota a wheel) + It.
monte a mountain, L. mons. See Rotary, Mount, n.]

Defn: A vain or blustering boaster; a braggart; a braggadocio. Sir T.
Herbert.
RODOMONT

=====
Rod"o*mont, a.

Defn: Bragging; vainly boasting.
