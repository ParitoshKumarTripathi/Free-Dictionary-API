SHOGUN
Sho"gun, n. Etym: [Chin. tsiang ki√ún commander in chief.]

Defn: A title originally conferred by the Mikado on the military
governor of the eastern provinces of Japan. By gradual usurpation of
power the Shoguns (known to foreigners as Tycoons) became finally the
virtual rulers of Japan. The title was abolished in 1867. [Written
variously, Shiogun, Shiogoon, etc.]
