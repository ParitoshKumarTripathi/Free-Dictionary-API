TRANSFIX
Trans*fix", v. t. [imp. & p. p. Transfixed; p. pr. & vb. n.
Transfixing.] Etym: [L. transfixus, p. p. of transfigure to transfix;
trans across, through + figere to fix, fasten. See Fix.]

Defn: To pierce through, as with a pointed weapon; to impale; as, to
transfix one with a dart.
