UNBISHOP
Un*bish"op, v. t. Etym: [1st pref. un- + bishop.]

Defn: To deprive, as a city, of a bishop; to deprive, as a clergyman,
of episcopal dignity or rights. [R.] "Then he unbishops himself."
Milton.
