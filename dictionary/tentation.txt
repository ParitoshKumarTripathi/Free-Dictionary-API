TENTATION
Ten*ta"tion, n. Etym: [L. tentatio: cf. F. tentation. See
Temptation.]

1. Trial; temptation. [Obs.] Sir T. Browne.

2. (Mech.)

Defn: A mode of adjusting or operating by repeated trials or
experiments. Knight.
