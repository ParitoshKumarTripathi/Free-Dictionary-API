TETRABRANCHIATA
Tet`ra*bran`chi*a"ta, n. pl. Etym: [NL. See Tetra-, and Branchia.]
(Zo√∂l.)

Defn: An order of Cephalopoda having four gills. Among living species
it includes only the pearly nautilus. Numerous genera and species are
found in the fossil state, such as Ammonites, Baculites, Orthoceras,
etc.
