PYROCHLORE
Pyr"o*chlore, n. Etym: [Pyro- + Gr. (Min.)

Defn: A niobate of calcium, cerium, and other bases, occurring
usually in octahedrons of a yellowish or brownish color and resinous
luster; -- so called from its becoming grass-green on being subjected
to heat under the blowpipe.
