HANDICRAFT
Hand"i*craft, n. Etym: [For handcraft, influenced by handiwork; AS.
handcræft.]

1. A trade requiring skill of hand; manual occupation; handcraft.
Addison.

2. A man who earns his living by handicraft; a handicraftsman. [R.]
Dryden.
