RELICT
Rel"ict (-kt), n. Etym: [L. relicta, fr. of relictus, p. p. of
relinquere to leave behind. See Relinquish.]

Defn: A woman whose husband is dead; a widow.
Eli dying without issue, Jacob was obbliged by law to marry his
relict, and so to raise up seed to his brother Eli. South.
