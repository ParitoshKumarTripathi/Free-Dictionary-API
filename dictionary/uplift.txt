UPLIFT
Up*lift", v. t. [imp. & p. p. Uplifting.]

Defn: To lift or raise aloft; to raise; to elevate; as, to uplift the
arm; to uplift a rock. Cowper.
Satan, talking to his nearest mate, With head uplift above the wave,
and eyes That sparkling blazed. Milton.
UPLIFT

=====
Up"lift`, n. (Geol.)

Defn: A raising or upheaval of strata so as to disturb their
regularity and uniformity, and to occasion folds, dislocations, and
the like.
