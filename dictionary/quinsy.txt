QUINSY
Quin"sy, n. Etym: [Contr. fr. squinancy, F. esquinancie, L. cynanche
a sort of sore throat, Gr. synanche sore throat, Gr. Hound, Anger,
and Cynanche.] (Med.)

Defn: An inflammation of the throat, or parts adjacent, especially of
the fauces or tonsils, attended by considerable swelling, painful and
impeded deglutition, and accompanied by inflammatory fever. It
sometimes creates danger of suffocation; -- called also squinancy,
and squinzey.
