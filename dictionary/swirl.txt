SWIRL
Swirl, v. t. & i. [imp. & p. p. Swirled; p. pr. & vb. n. Swirling.]
Etym: [Akin to Norw. svirla to whirl, freq. of sverra to whirl, Dan.
svirre, G. schwirren to whiz, to buzz. *177. See Swarm, n.]

Defn: To whirl, or cause to whirl, as in an eddy. "The river swirled
along." C. Kingsley.
SWIRL

=====
Swirl, n.

Defn: A whirling motion; an eddy, as of water; a whirl. "The silent
swirl of bats." Mrs. Browning.
