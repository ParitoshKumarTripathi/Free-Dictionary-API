THERAPEUTAE
Ther`a*peu"tæ, n. pl. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. Therapeutic.] (Eccl. Hist.)

Defn: A name given to certain ascetics said to have anciently dwelt
in the neighborhood of Alexandria. They are described in a work
attributed to Philo, the genuineness and credibility of which are now
much discredited.
