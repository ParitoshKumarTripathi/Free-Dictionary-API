RATAFIA
Rat`a*fi"a, n. Etym: [F., fr. Malay arak arrack + tafia a spirit
distilled from molasses.]

Defn: A spirituous liquor flavored with the kernels of cherries,
apricots, peaches, or other fruit, spiced, and sweetened with sugar;
-- a term applied to the liqueurs called noyau, curaçao, etc.
[Written also ratifia and ratafee.]
