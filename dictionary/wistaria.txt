WISTARIA
Wis*ta"ri*a, n. Etym: [NL.] Etym: [So named after Caspar Wistar, an
American anatomist.] (Bot.)

Defn: A genus of climbing leguminous plants bearing long, pendulous
clusters of pale bluish flowers.

Note: The species commonest in cultivation is the Wistaria Sinensis
from Eastern Asia. W. fruticosa grows wild in the southern parts of
the United States.
