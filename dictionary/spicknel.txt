SPICKNEL
Spick"nel, n. Etym: [Contr. from spike nail a large, long nail; -- so
called in allusion to the shape of its capillary leaves.] (Bot.)

Defn: An umbelliferous herb (Meum Athamanticum) having finely divided
leaves, common in Europe; -- called also baldmoney, mew, and
bearwort. [Written also spignel.]
