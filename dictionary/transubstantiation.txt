TRANSUBSTANTIATION
Tran`sub*stan`ti*a"tion, n. Etym: [LL. transubstantiatio: cf. F.
transsubstantiation.]

1. A change into another substance.

2. (R. C. Theol.)

Defn: The doctrine held by Roman Catholics, that the bread and wine
in the Mass is converted into the body and blood of Christ; --
distinguished from consubstantiation, and impanation.
