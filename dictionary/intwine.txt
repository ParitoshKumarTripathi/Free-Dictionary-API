INTWINE
In*twine", v. t. Etym: [Cf. Entwine.]

Defn: To twine or twist into, or together; to wreathe; as, a wreath
of flowers intwined. [Written also entwine.]
INTWINE

=====
In*twine", v. i.

Defn: To be or to become intwined.
