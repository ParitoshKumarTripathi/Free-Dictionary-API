UPSILON
Up"si*lon, n. [Gr. 'y^ psilo`n bare, mere, simple y.]

Defn: The 20th letter (U, u) of the Greek alphabet, a vowel having
originally the sound of oo as in room, becoming before the 4th
century b. c. that French u or Ger. ü. Its equivalent in English is u
or y.
