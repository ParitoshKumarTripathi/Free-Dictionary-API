RILL
Rill, n. Etym: [Cf. LG. rille a small channel or brook, a furrow, a
chamfer, OE. rigol a small brook, F. rigole a trench or furrow for
water, W. rhill a row, rhigol a little ditch. sq. root11.]

1. A very small brook; a streamlet.

2. (Astron.)

Defn: See Rille.
RILL

=====
Rill, v. i.

Defn: To run a small stream. [R.] Prior.
