RHACHIGLOSSA
Rhach`i*glos"sa, n.pl. Etym: [NL. See Rhachis, and Glossa.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: A division of marine gastropods having a retractile proboscis
and three longitudinal rows of teeth on the radula. It includes many
of the large ornamental shells, as the miters, murices, olives,
purpuras, volutes, and whelks. See Illust. in Append.
