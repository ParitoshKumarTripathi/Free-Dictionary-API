SCRIMP
Scrimp, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Scrimped; p. pr. & vb. n. Scrimping.]
Etym: [Cf. Dan. skrumpe, G. schrumpfen, D. krimpen. Cf. Shrimp,
Shrine.]

Defn: To make too small or short; to limit or straiten; to put on
short allowance; to scant; to contract; to shorten; as, to scrimp the
pattern of a coat.
SCRIMP

=====
Scrimp, a.

Defn: Short; scanty; curtailed.
SCRIMP

=====
Scrimp, n.

Defn: A pinching miser; a niggard. [U.S.]
