TAENIOGLOSSA
Tæ`ni*o*glos"sa, n. pl. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr. (Zoöl.)

Defn: An extensive division of gastropod mollusks in which the
odontophore is long and narrow, and usually bears seven rows of
teeth. It includes a large number of families both marine and fresh-
water.
