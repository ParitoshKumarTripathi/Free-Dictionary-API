PHYSIOPHYLY
Phys`i*oph"y*ly, n. Etym: [Gr. (Biol.)

Defn: The tribal history of the functions, or the history of the
paleontological development of vital activities, -- being a branch of
phylogeny. See Morphophyly. Haeckel.
