RHEUMATIC
Rheu*mat"ic, a. Etym: [Gr. rheumaticus, F. rhumatique. See Rheum,
Rheumatism.]

1. Derived from, or having the character of, rheum; rheumic. [Obs.]

2. (Med.)

Defn: Of or pertaining to rheumatism; as, rheumatic pains or
affections; affected with rheumatism; as, a rheumatic old man;
causing rheumatism; as, a rheumatic day.
That rheumatic diseases do abound. Shak.
RHEUMATIC

=====
Rheu*mat"ic, n.

Defn: One affected with rheumatism.
