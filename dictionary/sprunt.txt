SPRUNT
Sprunt, v. i. Etym: [Cf. Sprout, v. i.]

Defn: To spring up; to germinate; to spring forward or outward.
[Obs.] To sprunt up, to draw one's self up suddenly, as in anger or
defiance; to bristle up. [Local, U.S.]
SPRUNT

=====
Sprunt, n.

1. Anything short and stiff. [Obs.]

2. A leap; a spring. [Obs. or Prov. Eng.]

3. A steep ascent in a road. [Prov. Eng.]
SPRUNT

=====
Sprunt, a.

Defn: Active; lively; vigorous. [Obs.] Kersey.
