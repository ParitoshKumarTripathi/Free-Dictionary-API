GLEET
Gleet, n. Etym: [OE. glette, glet, glat, mucus, pus, filth, OF.
glete.] (Med.)

Defn: A transparent mucous discharge from the membrane of the
urethra, commonly an effect of gonorrhea. Hoblyn.
GLEET

=====
Gleet, v. i.

1. To flow in a thin, limpid humor; to ooze, as gleet. Wiseman.

2. To flow slowly, as water. Cheyne.
