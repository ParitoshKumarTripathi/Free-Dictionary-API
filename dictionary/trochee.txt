TROCHEE
Tro"chee, n. Etym: [L. trochaeus, Gr. Troche, Truck a wheel.] (Pros.)

Defn: A foot of two syllables, the first long and the second short,
as in the Latin word ante, or the first accented and the second
unaccented, as in the English word motion; a choreus.
