Knoll, n. Etym: [AS. cnoll; akin to G. knolle, knollen, clod, lump,

Defn: A little round hill; a mound; a small elevation of earth; the

On knoll or hillock rears his crest, Lonely and huge, the giant oak.
Sir W. Scott.
KNOLL

=====
Knoll, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Knolled; p. pr. & vb. n. Knolling.] Etym:
[OE. knollen, AS. cnyllan. See Knell.]

Defn: To ring, as a bell; to strike a knell upon; to toll; to
proclaim, or summon, by ringing. "Knolled to church." Shak.
Heavy clocks knolling the drowsy hours. Tennyson.
KNOLL

=====
Knoll, v. i.

Defn: To sound, as a bell; to knell. Shak.
For a departed being's soul The death hymn peals, and the hollow
bells knoll. Byron.
KNOLL

=====
Knoll, n.

Defn: The tolling of a bell; a knell. [R.] Byron.
