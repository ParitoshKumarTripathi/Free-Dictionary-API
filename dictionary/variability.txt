VARIABILITY
Va`ri*a*bil"i*ty, n. Etym: [Cf. F. variabilité.]

1. The quality or state of being variable; variableness.

2. (Biol.)

Defn: The power possessed by living organisms, both animal and
vegetable, of adapting themselves to modifications or changes in
their environment, thus possibly giving rise to ultimate variation of
structure or function.
