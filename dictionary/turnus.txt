TURNUS
Tur"nus, n. Etym: [NL., fr. L. Turnus, the king of the Rutuli,
mentioned in the Æneid.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: A common, large, handsome, American swallowtail butterfly, now
regarded as one of the forms of Papilio, or Jasoniades, glaucus. The
wings are yellow, margined and barred with black, and with an orange-
red spot near the posterior angle of the hind wings. Called also
tiger swallowtail. See Illust. under Swallowtail.
