PUNT
Punt, v. i. Etym: [F. ponter, or It. puntare, fr. L. punctum point.
See Point.]

Defn: To play at basset, baccara, faro. or omber; to gamble.
She heard . . . of his punting at gaming tables. Thackeray.
PUNT

=====
Punt, n.

Defn: Act of playing at basset, baccara, faro, etc.
PUNT

=====
Punt, n. Etym: [AS., fr. L. ponto punt, pontoon. See Pontoon.]
(Naut.)

Defn: A flat-bottomed boat with square ends. It is adapted for use in
shallow waters.
PUNT

=====
Punt, v. t.

1. To propel, as a boat in shallow water, by pushing with a pole
against the bottom; to push or propel (anything) with exertion.
Livingstone.

2. (Football)

Defn: To kick (the ball) before it touches the ground, when let fall
from the hands.
PUNT

=====
Punt, n. (Football)

Defn: The act of punting the ball.
