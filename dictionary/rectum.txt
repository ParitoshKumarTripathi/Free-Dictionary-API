RECTUM
Rec"tum (-tm), n. Etym: [NL. (sc. intestinum), fr. L. rectus
straight. See Right.] (Anat.)

Defn: The terminal part of the large intestine; -- so named because
supposed by the old anatomists to be straight. See Illust. under
Digestive.
