TAURUS
Tau"rus, n. Etym: [L., akin to Gr. steer. See Steer a young ox.]

1. (Astron.)
(a) The Bull; the second in order of the twelve signs of the zodiac,
which the sun enters about the 20th of April; -- marked thus [taurus]
in almanacs.
(b) A zodiacal constellation, containing the well-known clusters
called the Pleiades and the Hyades, in the latter of which is
situated the remarkably bright Aldebaran.

2. (Zoöl.)

Defn: A genus of ruminants comprising the common domestic cattle.
