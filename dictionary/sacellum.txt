SACELLUM
Sa*cel"lum, n.; pl. Sacella. Etym: [L., dim. of sacrum a sacred
place.]
(a) (Rom. Antiq.) An unroofed space consecrated to a divinity.
(b) (Eccl.) A small monumental chapel in a church. Shipley.
