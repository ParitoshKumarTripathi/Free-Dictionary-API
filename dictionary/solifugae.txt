SOLIFUGAE
So*lif"u*gæ, n. pl. Etym: [NL., from L. solifuga (better solipuga), a
kind of venomous ant, or spider.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: A division of arachnids having large, powerful fangs and a
segmented abdomen; -- called also Solpugidea, and Solpugides.
