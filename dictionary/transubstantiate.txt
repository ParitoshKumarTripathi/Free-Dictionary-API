TRANSUBSTANTIATE
Tran`sub*stan"ti*ate, v. t. Etym: [LL. transubstantiatus, p. p. of
transubstantiare to transubstantiate; L. trans across, over +
substantia substance. See Substance.]

1. To change into another substance. [R.]
The spider love which transubstantiates all, And can convert manna to
gall. Donne.

2. (R. C. Theol.)

Defn: To change, as the sacramental elements, bread and wine, into
the flesh and blood of Christ.
