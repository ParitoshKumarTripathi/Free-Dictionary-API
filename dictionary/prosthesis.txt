PROSTHESIS
Pros"the*sis, n. Etym: [L., fr. Gr.

1. (Surg.)

Defn: The addition to the human body of some artificial part, to
replace one that is wanting, as a log or an eye; -- called also
prothesis.

2. (Gram.)

Defn: The prefixing of one or more letters to the beginning of a
word, as in beloved.
