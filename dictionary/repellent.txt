REPELLENT
Re*pel"lent (-lent), a. Etym: [L. repellens, -entis, p. pr. ]

Defn: Driving back; able or tending to repel.
REPELLENT

=====
Re*pel"lent, n.

1. That which repels.

2. (Med.)

Defn: A remedy to repel from a tumefied part the fluids which render
it tumid. Dunglison.

3. A kind of waterproof cloth. Knight.
