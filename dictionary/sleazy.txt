SLEAZY
Slea"zy, a. Etym: [Cf. G. schleissig worn out, threadbare, from
schleissen to slit, split, decay, or E. leasy.]

Defn: Wanting firmness of texture or substance; thin; flimsy; as,
sleazy silk or muslin. [Spelt also slazy.]
