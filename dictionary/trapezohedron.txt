TRAPEZOHEDRON
Trap`e*zo*he"dron, n. Etym: [NL., from trapezium + Gr. (Crystalloq.)
(a) A solid bounded by twenty-four equal and similar trapeziums; a
tetragonal trisoctahedron. See the Note under Trisoctahedron.
(b) A tetartohedral solid of the hexagonal system, bounded by six
trapezoidal planes. The faces of this form are common on quartz
crystals.
