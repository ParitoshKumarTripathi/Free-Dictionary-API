SUFFLAMINATE
Suf*flam"i*nate, v. t. Etym: [L. sufflaminatus, p.p. of sufflaminare
to hold back by a clog, from sufflamen a clog.]

1. To retard the motion of, as a carriage, by preventing one or more
of its wheels from revolving, either by means of a chain or
otherwise. [Obs.]

2. Hence, to stop; to impede. [Obs.] Barrow.
