RORQUAL
Ror"qual, n. Etym: [Norw. rorqualus a whale with folds.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: A very large North Atlantic whalebone whale (Physalus
antiquorum, or Balænoptera physalus). It has a dorsal fin, and strong
longitudinal folds on the throat and belly. Called also razorback.

Note: It is one of the largest of the whales, somethimes becoming
nearly one hundred feet long, but it is more slender than the right
whales, and is noted for its swiftness. The name is sometimes applied
to other related species of finback whales.
