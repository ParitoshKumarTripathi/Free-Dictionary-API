TOTE
Tote, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Toted; p. pr. & vb. n. Toting.] Etym: [Said
to be of African origin.]

Defn: To carry or bear; as, to tote a child over a stream; -- a
colloquial word of the Southern States, and used esp. by negroes.
TOTE

=====
Tote, n. Etym: [L. totum, fr.totus all, whole.]

Defn: The entire body, or all; as, the whole tote. [Colloq.]
