WAMBLE
Wam"ble, v. i. Etym: [Cf. Dan. vamle, and vammel squeamish, ready to
vomit, Icel. væma to feel nausea, væminn nauseous.]

1. To heave; to be disturbed by nausea; -- said of the stomach.
L'Estrange.

2. To move irregularly to and fro; to roll.
WAMBLE

=====
Wam"ble, n.

Defn: Disturbance of the stomach; a feeling of nausea. Holland.
