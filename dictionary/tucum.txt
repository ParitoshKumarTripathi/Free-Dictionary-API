TUCUM
Tu"cum, n. Etym: [So called by the Indians of Brazil.]

Defn: A fine, strong fiber obtained from the young leaves of a
Brazilian palm (Astrocaryum vulgare), used for cordage, bowstrings,
etc.; also, the plant yielding this fiber. Called also tecum, and
tecum fiber.
