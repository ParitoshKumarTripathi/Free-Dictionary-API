SEQUESTRATOR
Seq"ues*tra`tor, n. Etym: [L., one that hinders or impedes.] (Law)
(a) One who sequesters property, or takes the possession of it for a
time, to satisfy a demand out of its rents or profits.
(b) One to whom the keeping of sequestered property is committed.
