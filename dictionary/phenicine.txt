PHENICINE
Phen"i*cine, n. Etym: [Gr. foi^nix purple red: cf. F. phénicine.]
(Chem.)
(a) A purple powder precipitated when a sulphuric solution of indigo
is diluted with water.
(b) A coloring matter produced by the action of a mixture of strong
nitric and sulphuric acids on phenylic alcohol. Watts.
