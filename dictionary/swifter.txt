SWIFTER
Swift"er, n. Etym: [Cf. Swivel.] (Naut.)
(a) A rope used to retain the bars of the capstan in their sockets
while men are turning it.
(b) A rope used to encircle a boat longitudinally, to strengthen and
defend her sides.
(c) The forward shroud of a lower mast.
SWIFTER

=====
Swift"er, v. t. (Naut.)

Defn: To tighten, as slack standing rigging, by bringing the opposite
shrouds nearer.
