SUFFOCATION
Suf`fo*ca"tion, n. Etym: [L. suffocatio: cf. F. suffocation.]

Defn: The act of suffocating, or the state of being suffocated; death
caused by smothering or choking.

Note: The term suffocation is sometimes employed synonymously with
asphyxia. In the strict medico-legal sense it signifies asphyxia
induced by obstruction of the respiration otherwise than by direct
pressure on the neck (hanging, strangulation) or submersion
(drowning). Quain.
