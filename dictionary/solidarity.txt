SOLIDARITY
Sol`i*dar"i*ty, n. Etym: [F. solidarité, fr. solide. See Solid.]

Defn: An entire union or consolidation of interests and
responsibilities; fellowship; community.
Solidarity [a word which we owe to the French Communists], signifies
a fellowship in gain and loss, in honor and dishonor, in victory and
defeat, a being, so to speak, all in the same boat. Trench.
The solidarity . . . of Breton and Welsh poetry. M. Arnold.
