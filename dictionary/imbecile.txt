IMBECILE
Im"be*cile, a. Etym: [L. imbecillis, and imbecillus; of unknown
origin: cf. F. imbécile.]

Defn: Destitute of strength, whether of body or mind; feeble;
impotent; esp., mentally wea; feeble-minded; as, hospitals for the
imbecile and insane.

Syn.
 -- Weak; feeble; feeble-minded; idiotic.
IMBECILE

=====
Im"be*cile, n.

Defn: One destitute of strength; esp., one of feeble mind.
IMBECILE

=====
Im"be*cile, v. t.

Defn: To weaken; to make imbecile; as, to imbecile men's courage.
[Obs.] Jer. Taylor.
