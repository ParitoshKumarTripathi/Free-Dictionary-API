QUARTERN
Quar"tern, n.Etym: [OE. quarteroun, quartron, F. quarteron, the
fourth part of a pound, or of a hundred; cf. L. quartarius a fourth
part, quarter of any measure, quartern, gill. See Quarter, and cf.
Quarteron, Quadroon.]

1. A quarter. Specifically: (a) The fourth part of a pint; a gill.
(b) The fourth part of a peck, or of a stone (14 ibs.).

2. A loaf of bread weighing about four pounds; -- called also
quartern loaf. Simmonds.
