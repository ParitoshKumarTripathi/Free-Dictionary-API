TRESAYLE
Tres"ayle` (três"al`), n. Etym: [F. trisaïeul, from L. tris, tres,
three + F. aïeul grandfather. Cf. Besaiel, and see Ayle.]

Defn: A grandfather's grandfather. [Obs.] Writ of tresayle (O. Eng.
Law), a writ which lay for a man claiming as heir to his
grandfather's grandfather, to recover lands of which he had been
deprived by an abatement happening on the ancestor's death. Mozley &
W.
