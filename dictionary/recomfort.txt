RECOMFORT
Re*com"fort (r*km"frt), v. t. Etym: [Pref. re- + comfort: cf. F.
réconforter.]

Defn: To comfort again; to console anew; to give new strength to.
Bacon.
Gan her recomfort from so sad affright. Spenser.
