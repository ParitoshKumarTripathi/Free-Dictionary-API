GLOBIGERINA
Glo*big`e*ri"na, n.; pl. Globigerinæ. Etym: [NL., fr. L. globus a
round body + gerere to bear.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: A genus of small Foraminifera, which live abundantly at or near
the surface of the sea. Their dead shells, falling to the bottom,
make up a large part of the soft mud, generally found in depths below
3,000 feet, and called globigerina ooze. See Illust. of Foraminifera.
