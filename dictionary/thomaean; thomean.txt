THOMAEAN; THOMEAN
Tho*mæ"an, Tho*me"an, n. (Eccl. Hist.)

Defn: A member of the ancient church of Christians established on the
Malabar coast of India, which some suppose to have been originally
founded by the Apostle Thomas.
