WANION
Wan"ion, n. Etym: [Probably for OE. waniand waning, p. pr. of wanien;
hence, used of the waning of the moon, supposed to be an unlucky
time. See Wane.]

Defn: A word of uncertain signification, used only in the phrase with
a wanion, apparently equivalent to with a vengeance, with a plague,
or with misfortune. [Obs.] B. Jonson. Latimer.
