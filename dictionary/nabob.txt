NABOB
Na"bob, n. Etym: [Hind. nawab, from Ar. nawab, pl. of na\'8bb a
vicegerent, governor. Cf Nawab.]

1. A deputy or viceroy in India; a governor of a province of the
ancient Mogul empire.

2. One who returns to Europe from the East with immense riches:
hence, any man of great wealth. " A bilious old nabob." Macaulay.
