QUATERNARY
Qua*ter"na*ry, a. Etym: [L. quaternarius consisting of four each,
containing four, fr. quaterni four each, fr. quattuor four: cf. F.
quaternaire. See Four.]

1. Consisting of four; by fours, or in sets of four.

2. (Geol.)

Defn: Later than, or subsequent to, the Tertiary; Post-tertiary; as,
the Quaternary age, or Age of man.
QUATERNARY

=====
Qua*ter"na*ry, n. Etym: [L. numerus quaternarius: cf. F.
quaternaire.]

1. The number four. Boyle.

2. (Geol.)

Defn: The Quaternary age, era, or formation. See the Chart of
Geology.
