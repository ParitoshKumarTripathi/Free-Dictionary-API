PACHYDERMATA
Pach`y*der"ma*ta, n. pl. [NL., fr. Gr.  thick-skinned; pachy`s thick
+  skin.] (Zoöl.)

Defn: A group of hoofed mammals distinguished for the thickness of
their skins, including the elephant, hippopotamus, rhinoceros, tapir,
horse, and hog. It is now considered an artificial group.
