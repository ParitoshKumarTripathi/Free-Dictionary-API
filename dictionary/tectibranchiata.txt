TECTIBRANCHIATA
Tec`ti*bran`chi*a"ta, n. pl. Etym: [NL., fr. L. tectus (p.p. of
tegere to cover) + Gr. (Zo√∂l.)

Defn: An order, or suborder, of gastropod Mollusca in which the gills
are usually situated on one side of the back, and protected by a fold
of the mantle. When there is a shell, it is usually thin and delicate
and often rudimentary. The aplysias and the bubble shells are
examples.
