GYNANDROMORPHISM
Gy*nan"dro*mor`phism, n. Etym: [Gr. gynh` a woman, female + (Zoöl.)

Defn: An abnormal condition of certain animals, in which one side has
the external characters of the male, and the other those of the
female.
