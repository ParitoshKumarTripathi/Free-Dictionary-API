TETRAGONAL
Te*trag"o*nal, a.

1. (Geom.)

Defn: Of or pertaining to a tetragon; having four angles or sides;
thus, the square, the parallelogram, the rhombus, and the trapezium
are tetragonal fingers.

2. (Bot.)

Defn: Having four prominent longitudinal angles.

3. (Crystallog.)

Defn: Designating, or belonging to, a certain system of
crystallization; dimetric. See Tetragonal system, under
Crystallization.
