REPUBLICANIZE
Re*pub"lic*an*ize (-z), v. t. [imp. & p. p. Republicanized (-zd); p.
pr. & vb. n. Republicanizing (-`zng).] Etym: [Cf. F. républicaniser.]

Defn: To change, as a state, into a republic; to republican
principles; as, France was republicanized; to republicanize the
rising generation. D. Ramsay.
