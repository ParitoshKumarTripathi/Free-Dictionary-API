SCLEROTIUM
Scle*ro"ti*um, n.; pl. Sclerotia. Etym: [NL., fr. Gr.

1. (Bot.)

Defn: A hardened body formed by certain fungi, as by the Claviceps
purpurea, which produced ergot.

2. (Zoöl.)

Defn: The nature or resting stage of a plasmodium.
