PSEUDO-PERIPTERAL
Pseu`do-pe*rip"ter*al, a. Etym: [Pseudo- + peripteral: cf. F.
pseudopériptère.] (Arch.)

Defn: Falsely or imperfectly peripteral, as a temple having the
columns at the sides attached to the walls, and an ambulatory only at
the ends or only at one end.
 -- n.

Defn: A pseudo-peripteral temple. Oxf. Gloss.
