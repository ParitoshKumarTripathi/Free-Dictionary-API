UTTERMOST
Ut"ter*most, a. Etym: [From Utter, a.; cf. Utmost, and Outermost.]

Defn: Extreme; utmost; being; in the farthest, greatest, or highest
degree; as, the uttermost extent or end. "In this uttermost
distress." Milton.
UTTERMOST

=====
Ut"ter*most`, n.

Defn: The utmost; the highest or greatest degree; the farthest
extent. Tennyson.
Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come
unto God by him. Heb. vii. 25.
He cannot have sufficient honor done unto him; but the uttermost we
can do, we must. Hooker.
