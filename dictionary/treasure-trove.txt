TREASURE-TROVE
Treas"ure-trove`, n. Etym: [Treasure + OF. trové, F. trouvé, p. p. of
OF. trover to find, F. trouver. See Trover.] (Common Law)

Defn: Any money, bullion, or the like, found in the earth, or
otherwise hidden, the owner of which is not known. In England such
treasure belongs to the crown; whereas similar treasure found in the
sea, or upon the surface of the land, belongs to the finder if no
owner appears.
