PHONOSCOPE
Pho"no*scope, n. Etym: [Phono- + -scope.] (Physics)
(a) An instrument for observing or exhibiting the motions or
properties of sounding bodies; especially, an apparatus invented by
König for testing the quality of musical strings.
(b) An instrument for producing luminous figures by the vibrations of
sounding bodies.
