OENANTHOL
OE*nan"thol, n. Etym: [oenanthylic + L. oleum oil.] (Chem.)

Defn: An oily substance obtained by the distillation of castor oil,
recognized as the aldehyde of oenanthylic acid, and hence called also
oenanthaldehyde.
